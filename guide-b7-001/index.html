Lab Note B7-001
Parameter Riding · 01 — Overview

Riding the few parameters that matter, live

A deep reference for performing a hypnotic techno track in real time — choosing the four or five controls you hold under your hands, mapping them to a hardware surface, deciding what to ride and what to leave fixed, and rehearsing a take you can actually play. Inspired by the discipline of live techno, not by any one set. Your performance must be your own.

The goal

A performed take, not a render

  • The same track from Lab Note B1-001, but its energy arc played by hand instead of drawn
  • Four mapped continuous controls plus mutes — nothing more under your fingers
  • A recorded ride you could repeat in front of a room, captured as Arrangement audio

Skills you'll practice

  • MIDI-mapping a FaderFox EC4, Push, or Launchpad to the controls that carry the arc
  • Setting Min/Max ranges so a mapped value never reaches a sound that hurts
  • Hands versus automation: when to ride by feel, when to delegate to a curve
  • Rehearsing gestures slow enough that the room feels change without hearing it
Core objective

Understand that a performance is a system you ride — and that control comes from mapping few things well, not from reaching for everything at once.

Why this exercise matters

The beginner instinct

  • Map every knob within reach, so nothing is ever out of control
  • Ride everything at once, twisting controls because they are there
  • Treat the master and the kick as performance toys

The opposite discipline

  • Map four controls you can watch, recover, and name from memory
  • Ride slower than feels natural; let the machines run their own drift
  • Leave the dangerous things — master, kick — fixed on purpose

The skills transfer. The same instincts apply to ambient sets, modular jams, dub-techno performances, generative patches, and any live electronic music where your hands shape a system that is already running. You are not learning a controller — you are learning what is worth touching.

Principle

If a move must change with the room, ride it. If it must be the same every time, automate it. Riding everything is not control — it is panic with extra steps.
