Lab Note B5-001
Delay · 01 — Overview

Delay as architecture

A single-topic deep reference on delay — not as an insert you switch on, but as the load-bearing structure of a track. We build a dub-style piece where the delay is the composition: the dry parts are sparse, and the space between them is where the music actually happens.

The claim

Delay is a path, not a knob

  • Where the signal leaves, how much returns, where it sits in stereo, what it sounds like by the time you hear it again
  • Decide the routing before the sound — the sound is downstream of the route
  • Send vs insert is the first decision, and it defines everything after it

A dub track built around the throw

  • Few dry elements; the delay supplies the density
  • The send is a performance control — a throw, not a setting
  • Filtered feedback so the tail dissolves instead of piling into mud
Core objective

Make a 6–8 minute original where, if you muted the delay returns, less than half the track would remain. The delay is not decoration on top of an arrangement — it is the arrangement.

What you will practise

Routing as a decision

  • Send vs insert; return level vs feedback amount — the two-knob confusion
  • Sync'd note divisions vs free milliseconds and tape drift
  • Ping-pong width that survives the mono fold

Delay as gesture

  • Dub throws — spike the send for one hit, let it trail for bars
  • Filtered feedback so each repeat darkens and dies
  • Automating throws on Ableton, p-locking them on Digitakt II

This is one of the Space-as-Instrument block (block 5). Reverb and feedback are siblings; this note isolates delay and goes deep. The skills transfer wherever timing, repetition, and decay carry the emotion — dub techno, ambient, IDM, soundtrack, live performance.

Principle

Do less, and let the echo carry the rest. Restraint in the dry signal is what makes room for the delay to become structure.

  • Originality constraint. The reference dubs on the next page are listening targets, not sources. Study how the delay moves, then build something fully your own. No borrowed patterns, no copied throws — only the principle.
