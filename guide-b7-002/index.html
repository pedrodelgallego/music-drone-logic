Lab Note B7-002
Live Resampling · 01 — Overview

Build a set section that feeds on itself

A deep reference for the on-stage resampling loop: capturing a few bars of your own output mid-performance and replaying them as new material — quantised capture, transforms, round-trip latency, clock alignment, and a gain ceiling on the loop. Inspired by the practice of live resampling, not by any one performer's set. Your section must be fully original.

The goal

A self-feeding section

  • 4–8 bars of your live mix, captured quantised to the bar
  • Replayed transformed — reversed, pitched, chopped, frozen
  • Folded back into the mix as a new voice that could never clash

Fundamentals you'll practice

  • Quantised capture, one capture engine, committed routing
  • Transforms that make a capture read as new, not as a doubled mix
  • Round-trip latency compensation, clock alignment, gain discipline
Core objective

Understand how the output of a performance can become its own next input — and how to keep that loop musical instead of letting it run away.

Why this exercise matters

The beginner instinct

  • Capture whatever, whenever the hand is free
  • Replay it raw, on top of the source, at full level
  • Stack capture on capture until the mix turns to mud

The opposite discipline

  • Arm early, trigger on the bar, capture the densest moment
  • One deliberate transform per capture; one capture engine per set
  • A measured latency offset and a gain ceiling that holds the loop down

The skill transfers. The same loop — capture, transform, return, manage the gain — underpins live looping, generative systems, dub mixing, granular performance, and no-input feedback. You are not learning a trick; you are learning to operate a system that listens to itself.

Principle

The output becomes the input. Transform it, time it, and cap its gain — or the loop stops being music and becomes feedback.
