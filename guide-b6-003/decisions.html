Lab Note B6-003
Chance Operations · 03 — Decisions

Chance vs randomness, and where authorship lives

John Cage used coin tosses and the I Ching to make decisions he refused to make himself. The radical part was never the randomness — it was the boundary. Cage decided what chance would choose between; chance only picked. This page turns that distinction into the concrete decisions you make before touching the gear.

Indeterminacy is not randomness

These two words get used as synonyms and they are opposites in practice. Indeterminacy is a composed field whose outcome is undetermined at writing time. Randomness is the absence of a field. The same coin can serve either one — what differs is whether you built the fence first.

Indeterminacy (Cage)

  • The outcome is undetermined at the time of writing
  • The field of possible outcomes is fully composed
  • The author refuses ego-driven choice, not responsibility
  • Every result is one you'd have signed your name to

Mere randomness

  • No composed field — anything goes
  • The result carries no intention to read against
  • Sounds arbitrary because there was no boundary to violate
  • Most outcomes need rescuing before they're usable
Principle

Chance is a composer only when you have already decided what it is allowed to compose. Otherwise it is a noise generator wearing a philosophy.

The boundary is the composition

In Music of Changes, Cage built charts of durations, dynamics, and sounds, then consulted the I Ching to choose among them. The charts are the human composition; the coins are the executor. This is the same architecture as a Digitakt trig condition — author the options, hand off the pick. The interesting work happens when you draw the fence.

Human: fixed kick + key + form — never random — Boundary: the composed field → dice picks → Which outcome, this bar

The chance devices, and what fence each one wants

Pick a decision-maker and a boundary for it to act inside. The historical lineage runs from literal dice to running code, but the structure is identical: a fence, then a roll.

Chance device → boundary → who's in the room
Coin / dice (Mozart's Würfelspiel)Pre-composed bars; the dice only choose the order. The human wrote every bar — the tightest fence possible.
I Ching (Cage, Music of Changes)Charts of sounds and durations; the oracle picks cells. The chart is the composition.
Digitakt % conditionTwo outcomes — play or silence — on one authored trig. The smallest possible chance operation.
Digitakt A:B / NEI conditionsLogic conditions across pattern cycles — chance with a memory, not a fresh coin each bar.
VCV Bernoulli Gate / random CVA coin in the control path; route it to which sample plays, never to whether the kick exists.
Live 12 per-note chanceEach note carries its own probability inside a fixed clip — the field is the clip.
Algorithm (TidalCycles rand, SuperCollider Prand)Code chooses among a list you wrote. The list is the boundary; the function is the dice.

Worked example — chance chose the variation, you chose the song

Worked example

Draw the line between decided and rolled

Decided in advance — no chance touches these
  • Tempo 124 BPM, key A natural minor, the four-on-the-floor kick, the eight-minute form inherited from Lab Note B1-001.
  • The three notes the bass is allowed to use (A1, C2, G1) and the three steps it's allowed to land on.
  • That there is a peak, where it sits, and that nothing new enters during it.
Handed to the dice
  • Which of the three notes the bass plays this bar — a 3-way random CV in VCV, or a Prand([0,3,7]) in SuperCollider.
  • Whether the rare perc hit fires — one 35 % trig on the Digitakt.
  • Which of two texture samples the Digitakt loads on a conditional trig — a sample lock chosen by a coin.
  • The order of three pre-built 16-bar section variants — a die roll, Mozart-style.
Decision check
  • If you removed all the chance, would a coherent track remain? It must — chance decorates structure, it never is the structure.
  • Could you write down, right now, the complete list of things chance is choosing between? If not, you've crossed from indeterminacy into noise.
  • Does each roll feel like one of several things you'd have been happy with? That's the sign the boundary is well chosen.
  • Are you using the coin to avoid a specific decision, or to avoid deciding at all? Only the first is allowed.

Where chance helps, where it's just noise

Chance earns its place when

  • It chooses among options you'd each accept
  • It breaks a loop you could otherwise predict bar-for-bar
  • It removes a decision your ego keeps making the same safe way
  • The boundary is tight enough that every result is usable

Chance is just noise when

  • It decides whether core elements exist at all
  • The range is so wide every result needs fixing
  • You're using it to avoid making any decision, not a specific one
  • You keep the first roll because re-rolling is effort

How tight should the fence be?

Boundary width → what you get → the failure mode
Very tight (Mozart: 176 bars all in key)Every outcome works; surprise is mild but reliable. Best for the structural spine.
Moderate (3 allowed notes, 1 probability trig)Clear variation you can still recognise as your track. The sweet spot for this build.
Loose (any note in the scale, any step)Sometimes brilliant, often needs a re-roll. Acceptable only if you commit to curating.
Open (any pitch, any time — radios)The result carries no intention. Use only when chaos itself is the subject.
  • The honest tell: good chance operations make you curate (you run it many times and choose); lazy ones make you settle (you run it once and live with it). Curation is the build page and the reflection — it is the price of admission for using chance at all.
  • Re-roll budget: decide before you start how many rolls you'll audition per decision. "Best of three" is a discipline; "the first one is fine" is surrender.
Carry forward

Cage didn't abandon authorship to chance — he relocated it. The composition moved from "which note" to "which field of notes." Decide where your authorship lives, then let the coin work below it.
