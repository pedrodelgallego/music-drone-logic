Block 2 · Replication lesson

Replicate: capture sorted by physics

Copy one clear section of Bernard Parmegiani — De Natura Sonorum. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: sounds organized by physical behavior - incidence and resonance, accumulation, matter - rather than by what produced them; the movement titles name physics, not objects. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

Before you start
  • Gear: recorder or phone, or a mic into M4 input 1 with gain by the lab-001 ledger; DT 990 Pro for sorting and editing; the lab-006 capture log, with one extra column for behavior class
  • New set saved as REP-DE-NATURA — no tempo; the studies are timed in seconds, not bars
  • Strikes and pours spike fast — set gain on the hardest strike and the densest pour, not on room tone; phones at the lab-001 mark while auditioning, because rings and grains invite the knob up
  • Timebox: 90–120 minutes — one capture round indoors, one sort, three short studies, then file it and stop
  • What you will make: a short replica of Bernard Parmegiani — De Natura Sonorum. Focus on this effect: movements where unlike sources fuse into single studies because they behave alike - struck things ring together, grains pour together.
  • What you will turn off: extra parts, decoration, and shortcuts that hide whether the mechanism works.
  • What you will prove: your version and REF use the same core mechanism: sounds organized by physical behavior - incidence and resonance, accumulation, matter - rather than by what produced them; the movement titles name physics, not objects.
  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Bernard Parmegiani — De Natura Sonorum. Choose the clearest short section. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. What do the sounds inside one movement share?
    Answer

    Not a source - a behavior. One movement is struck things and their ringing, another is small events accumulating, another is matter rubbed and held. The membership rule is physical: how the energy arrives and decays.

  2. Can you name the sources?
    Answer

    Sometimes - and it stops mattering within seconds. Electronic tones and concrete captures sit side by side in the same study, and the class absorbs them both.

  3. What does “incidence/resonance” sound like?
    Answer

    A strike and what rings after - energy arriving in an instant, then living on in a body.

  4. What does accumulation sound like?
    Answer

    Rain logic: one small event is a tick, a thousand are a surface. Grains pile until the ear stops counting and starts hearing texture - density becomes the parameter, and individual identity dissolves into pour.

  5. Where is the composition, if the sounds are a taxonomy?
    Answer

    In the sorting and the seams. Choosing the classes, deciding what belongs in each, ordering the entries inside a class, and handing one class to the next - those decisions are the form.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
  • Sound target: three 30-second studies — one per behavior class — where unlike sources audibly cohere because their physics match.
  • 12–15 captures of your own; three classes: struck-and-ringing / granular pours / friction sustains.
  • Sort by behavior, never by source — sources mix freely inside a class and never define one.
  • Selection, editing, and level only — the coherence must come from the sorting, not from processing.

Phase 1 · ≈ 10 min

The reference and the class sheet

Phase focus: the reference and the class sheet. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live ⌘ T, rename the track REF (⌘ R), drag the record’s opening movement in, and loop it.
  2. Live Audio Effects → Utility, trim to ≈ −12 dBFS.
  3. Logbook Write the class sheet in the logbook, one line per class: STRUCK — hit once, rings after;
  4. Logbook POUR — many small events, heard as density; FRICTION — rubbed or dragged, energy held as long as the hand moves.
  5. Logbook List candidate captures under each class, aiming at 4–5 per class from what is in reach: strike a glass, a metal bowl, a radiator;
  6. Logbook pour rice, gravel, coins, cutlery; drag a chair, rub a wine glass rim, run a zip, drag a book across a table.
  7. Logbook One more line: the membership test — a capture joins a class by its envelope, never by its name.
Expected result

The record loops at ≈ −12 dBFS, three class definitions are on paper, and 12–15 candidates are listed by behavior before anything is recorded.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The candidate list groups by room — kitchen sounds, desk soundsSorting by source out of habitRewrite each entry as an envelope: “rings after,” “pours,” “held” — the room column disappears
The friction column is nearly emptyFriction sources do not announce themselves the way strikes doAnything that resists motion qualifies — chairs, zips, corks, wet glass; push something slowly and listen
Phase 2 · ≈ 30 min

Capture — hunt behaviors, not objects

Phase focus: capture — hunt behaviors, not objects. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Record 12–15 captures from the class sheet, close, with gain by the lab-001 ledger — set on the hardest strike for percussive takes, on the densest pour for the granular ones, peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.
  2. For each struck capture: one clean hit, then hold still until the ring dies completely.
  3. The decay is the capture.
  4. Live For each pour: 8–10 seconds of steady density — pour from a constant height, refill and repeat rather than letting it trail off.
  5. Live For each friction: 8–10 seconds of even pressure and speed.
  6. Live If it squeaks into pitch, even better — but constant, not gestural.
  7. Live Log each take with place · distance · level plus its class letter — the extra column is this lesson’s addition to the lab-006 log.
Expected result

12–15 clean captures, 4–5 per class, each performed for its behavior — full decays, steady pours, even sustains — and logged with class letters.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
Rings cut short on playbackYou moved, spoke, or stopped recording before the decay diedRe-take and count silently to five after the ring disappears; the tail is the material
Pours sound like single rattlesToo little material falling, or falling too brieflyMore grains, longer feed, harder surface — density needs supply
Phase 3 · ≈ 15 min

The sort — names off, envelopes on

Phase focus: the sort — names off, envelopes on. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Import everything into REP-DE-NATURA; trim each capture to its behavior — the hit-plus-full-ring, the steady core of the pour, the even stretch of the friction.
  2. Live Rename every clip class·number — STRUCK·1, POUR·3 — source names nowhere in the set.
  3. Live Lay the clips on three tracks, one per class.
  4. Live Audition each track top to bottom: does every clip belong with its neighbors as a shape?
  5. Live Re-file the misfits: a coin dropped once is a strike, not a pour, whatever the candidate list said.
  6. Live If you catch yourself sorting by what made the sound, re-sort by what it does.
  7. Logbook Note the borderline cases in the logbook — a capture that strikes then pours is tomorrow’s most interesting material, but today it gets filed by its dominant behavior.
Expected result

Three tracks of class-named clips whose envelopes match within each track — a taxonomy a stranger could verify by ear alone.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
One class holds nearly everythingThe capture round followed comfort — usually all strikesTen minutes of targeted re-capture for the thin classes; the studies need 4 per class minimum
Clips will not settle into any classTakes contain multiple behaviors in sequenceSplit at the behavior change — one clip, one envelope — and file the halves separately
Phase 4 · ≈ 35 min

Three studies — one class each, sources mixed freely

Phase focus: three studies — one class each, sources mixed freely. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live Build three 30-second studies in Arrangement, one per class — selection, editing, and level only.
  2. Live STRUCK study: strikes from different sources answering each other;
  3. Live try handing one strike’s attack to another’s ring at least once — the Parmegiani seam.
  4. Live POUR study: layer and stagger pours into one changing density — thin to thick to thin;
  5. Live the parameter you compose is how many grains per second the listener receives.
  6. Live FRICTION study: overlap sustains from unlike sources into one held band;
  7. Live let pressure changes inside the takes be the events.
  8. Live Mix sources as freely as you can inside each study — the more unlike the sources, the louder the lesson when they cohere anyway.
Expected result

Three studies, each a single coherent instrument built from unlike sources — taxonomy heard as composition, ninety seconds total.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
A study sounds like a list of objectsClips placed end to end with no overlap — sources never asked to fuseOverlap entries so one behavior hands to the next mid-flight; fusion needs contact
One clip keeps sticking out of its studyA mis-sorted envelope — the odd shape breaks the class’s spellTrust the ear over the file: re-file the clip or cut it; the study’s coherence outranks any single sound
Verify against the record

Solo-flip between REF and your studies at matched loudness — phones at the mark, no level riding while you judge.

  • Do unlike sources cohere because their physics match?
    Answer

    In a working study, yes — the glass and the radiator stop being a glass and a radiator and become one ringing instrument with two colors, exactly the way the record fuses electronic and concrete material inside one movement. If a study refuses to cohere, the cause is almost always one mis-sorted envelope, not a processing deficit — this lesson allows no processing precisely so the sort takes the credit or the blame. Relisten to the record’s opening movement and count how many different sources you can hear fusing; then count yours.

  • Could a listener name the class faster than the sources?
    Answer

    That is the pass condition. Play one study to fresh ears — or to yourself tomorrow — and ask “what is happening?” before “what made it?” A working study gets “things being struck” or “something pouring” in a second, while the source list takes guesswork. If the source name arrives first (“that’s rice”), the class was too thin or one source dominated; widen the sources inside the class. The record passes this test in every movement, and its titles — physics, not objects — tell you it was designed to.

  • Logbook — three-line miss list. Matched: where your version produces the record’s effect. Missed: where it doesn’t — and your best guess why. Change next: the one move you’d try first.
