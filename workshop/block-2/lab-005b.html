Block 2 · Lab 005b · Track lab — worked exemplar

The Beatless Long-Form

The drone strand's worked exemplar: an original 8–12 minute beatless piece, built step by step with every knob position and decision checkpoint spelled out. What Lab 003 is to the loop, this lab is to the drone — and it condenses Lab Note 002, which remains the deep reference.

  • Run Lab 005's seven listening references first — they are this lab's hypothesis source; do not repeat them here, reuse the worksheets.
  • Read Lab Note 002 end to end once before the first session — this page is the condensed path; the note carries the full reasoning.

The point

Lab 003 proved that a loop plus subtraction plus drift can carry eight minutes. This lab removes the loop and proves the harder claim: beating, slow modulation, and level can carry ten — with nothing repeating and nothing pulsing.

1 · Overview

One patch, four layers

  • Foundation, interval, and air are three printed passes of one saved MicroFreak patch.
  • The texture bed is that same patch sampled into the Digitakt II and eroded by resampling.
  • Coherence is structural — every layer is the same tone wearing different weight.

Beating is the pulse

  • The interval pass is detuned against the foundation by a chosen number of cents.
  • Detune in Hz equals beats per second — the pulse is tuned, not programmed.
  • Target 0.3–1.5 Hz; write the number in the spec before recording.

Level is the arrangement

  • No mutes, no entrances: four volume lanes and one reverb send are the entire form.
  • Every fade is sixty seconds or longer.
  • The "peak" is saturation density at constant level — mass, not loudness.

3 · The Held Tone Test — certified here

A recurring certification

Like the bench certification, the Held Tone Test renews at gates: 3 minutes here · 6 minutes at C2 · 10 minutes at C5 and C7. It is the drone strand's claim, made falsifiable and repeatable.

Certification

One tone, three minutes, documented

  1. Design and print one single held tone — one voice, no layers, no effects — that rewards three unbroken minutes of attention.
  2. Sit through it, eyes closed, phones at the mark. No skipping; the test is the sitting.
  3. File the take plus one paragraph naming where the life lives — which modulator, what rate, what it moves. "It sounds nice" fails; "the cycling envelope opens Wave over 40 seconds while the LFO stirs Timbre every 15" passes.
  4. Routing sketch: one line, source to ears, with the gain decision and where it was set (body, not swell).
Pass condition

You finished the three minutes wanting more rather than checking the clock — and the paragraph proves you know why, mechanically. If it fails, the fix is in the patch (rates, amounts, harmonic content), never in effects.

4 · Build — condensed from Lab Note 002

The full worked build, with every knob position, choreography table, and decision check, is Lab Note 002 — The build. The condensed path:

  1. Gridless project — metronome off, ruler in minutes, 48 kHz / 24-bit, buffer 512, auto-warp off. Tracks: FOUNDATION · INTERVAL · AIR · BED · return A — REVERB (8–12 s, 100 % wet).
  2. The voice — init patch, one engine committed (Harmo is the worked default), attack ≥2 s, full sustain, cutoff dark. The aliveness mechanism: LFO → Timbre (~25, unsynced, 15–20 s/cycle) and cycling env → Cutoff (~15, 30–60 s loop). Three-minute listen before judging. Save as LAB2 VOICE.
  3. Foundation take — ten minutes, Hold on the root, hands off for two minutes, at most three minute-long knob moves, drift home by the end. Gain on the body, peaks −15 to −12 dBFS.
  4. Interval take — same patch at the chosen interval; fine-tune against the looping foundation until the pulse sits at the spec's named rate. Ten minutes, hands off. Pan the pair ≤25 % apart; the beating must survive mono.
  5. Air + bed — air: +2 octaves, five minutes, HP 800 Hz, unsynced Echo ≤30 % feedback, fader −25 dB. Bed: sample 30–60 s of the foundation into the Digitakt II, one looped trig, −1 octave, slowest track LFO on cutoff, resample 1–2 generations, print five quiet minutes.
  6. Level choreography — the arrangement is four volume lanes + one send, planned on paper as "which layer is in front, per minute," every fade ≥60 s, S-curves or ridden takes (keep the calmer of two).
  7. Bloom and erosion — reverb send −15 → −6 dB across the middle; Saturator drive +2 → +8 dB on the bed in the final third, output compensated so density rises while the meter does not.
  8. Subtract and end — layers sink in reverse order of arrival; last 60–90 s foundation alone, master fade into the tail.
  9. Mix for sustain — registers by HP (foundation owns <200 Hz), mono check ×3, one full quiet listen, no limiter, peaks ≤ −3 dBFS.
  10. Render and step away — normalize off, listen elsewhere, ten-line notes file, one change next day, v2.
  • Prescribed fault — the static drone: zero both modulation amounts and print two minutes. A/B against the real patch at matched level and write the rule: name the smallest modulation amount at which the tone reads as alive. This is the lab's failure mode, manufactured on purpose so you recognize it when it happens by accident.

5 · Diagnosis & report

The full report bundle — ten items, filed in the logbook
  • Rendered piece — 24-bit WAV, master peaking −3 dBFS, 8–12 minutes
  • Routing diagram — drawn before patching, corrected after; the diff is the lesson
  • Recording plan — what was printed, when, at what stage
  • Patch notes — engine, modulator rates and amounts, the interval, and the beat rate in Hz
  • Session notes
  • Reflection answers, including the listening comparison — which Lab 005 reference contributed what
  • One removal decision — what was cut and what cutting it did
  • Fault entry — symptom, cause, fix, prevention rule
  • Print map — what exists as audio, automation, patch state, device memory; what is gone
  • System diff sentence — “I thought the system worked like X; after building it, I discovered it worked like Y.”
Pass standard
Minimum passThe bundle above, plus the Held Tone Test certified at 3 minutes.
Solid passMinute 1 and minute 5 are audibly different states; no change is catchable in the act; the beating survives mono; the erosion thickens while the meter holds.
Advanced transferYou can state what each Block 1 loop skill did and did not transfer — and rebuild the same slowness from a different engine or interval.
  1. Where does this piece's slowness live — and is that where your hypothesis said it would?
  2. What did the beating contribute that one modulated tone could not?
  3. Which level ride mattered most, and was it also the slowest?
  4. What did the Lab 003 skills mislead you about here?
  5. At which minute did your attention die during the full playback — and which layer's interior was parked when it did?
Worked system diff — the expected granularity

“I thought the piece would live in the layering; it lived in the detune — the layers were just where the beating happened.”

Track-lab principle

A drone is slow, not static — remove the clock and beating, modulation, and level must do all of its work.
