Block 7 — Performance & Curation

What the human does in real time

Riding, live resampling, audio feedback systems, the long form, and the performance rig — the operational engineering year.

Year 3 · Domain F · Labs 027–031 · Programme L7 · Capstone C6

Engineering focus

Every performance lab requires a live signal-flow diagram, a panic path (mute, limiter, bypass, stop, fallback loop), a failure rehearsal, a recording strategy, and a post-performance fault report. The question is not “did the set go well?” — it is “when the system became unstable, did you understand it well enough to recover musically?”

Listening programme L7 — performance practice

  • Records: live recordings of hardware sets — what was committed, what stayed live?
  • Samples: deconstruction — one live recording vs its studio version
  • Video: David Tudor archival footage · contemporary hardware live sets · rig breakdowns — watched for recovery from failure, not just flow
  • Deliverable: system analyses + one stolen principle, evaluated at C6 forthcoming
