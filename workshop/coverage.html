Coverage & gaps

A map of what this workshop teaches well, what it touches lightly, and what it leaves out — across every major topic and piece of gear. Each topic is scored two ways: how well the idea is taught, and whether you can actually practise it now.

The key idea

This course is concept-first: it teaches a system, then gives a free way to practise it. So a topic can score high on concept while the matching hardware is left out on purpose — and still be practisable, through a software stand-in.

Re-audited 2026-06-13 — all gaps closed

Every gap this assessment originally flagged is now closed. The fundamentals "atoms" layer is built (Block 00 — Foundations, all nine primers complete with proof gates), and the per-topic "What to add" items now ship as dedicated pages: sample hygiene, Digitakt hygiene, who sequences what, CC-mapping lab, controller map, session survival, M4 troubleshooting, synthesis index, drone pathway, room & monitoring, and the gear roadmap + purchase gate. The last two open items have now been resolved: a dedicated CV, Gate & Voltage Control page is written, and the five hardware integration pages are complete, gated lessons (each with key concepts and a proof gate) — not stubs. They stay locked behind the purchase gate by design, since hands-on work needs the gear, but the writing is done.

  • Two scores, 1–5. Concept = how well the idea/system is taught. Hands-on = whether you can practise it today (owned gear or a provided free substitute) and whether that practice is assessed. Where hardware is missing, the workshop supplies a free stand-in: VCV Rack for modular, Pure Data / SuperCollider / TidalCycles for generative systems, Scala for tuning, a DAW feedback loop for no-input mixing. "The system is the lesson; the substrate is interchangeable."
  • The "atoms" gap. The course teaches the advanced/systems layer of synthesis, rhythm, harmony, mixing, effects, and ear training while assuming the ground-up building blocks beneath them (audio engineering in Block 1 is the one exception, taught from scratch — which is why it scores 5/5). The fix is an optional fundamentals-primer layer (a "Lab Note 000" series).
  • The full assessment — the atoms-gap audit, the primer track, expanded notes, a ranked roadmap, cost/risk bands, and a gear purchase gate — lives in docs/equipment-and-concept-coverage.md.

The 1–5 scale

5 · Core spineReappears across blocks as a required operating skill, with proof.
4 · StrongDedicated pages/labs, repeated use, and a clear deliverable.
3 · PartialCovered through adjacent work or a free substitute; no dedicated lab or assessment.
2 · MentionedAppears as a term or option, but no student proof.
1 · MissingNo meaningful coverage or no way to practise — usually a hardware category left out by design.

The fundamentals ("atoms") layer

The biggest pattern in this audit: the course teaches the advanced/systems layer of each domain while assuming the beginner atoms beneath it. Audio engineering (Block 1) is the one exception — it teaches its atoms from scratch, which is why it scores 5/5. The fix is an optional "Lab Note 000" primer layer, every lesson anchored to gear you own. This layer is now built — see Block 00 — Foundations (start with Synthesis from Scratch).

All nine primers are built and complete. Each is a dedicated page that drills its atoms individually and ends in a stated proof gate. C/H scores below are the current (post-build) state — every row links to its page.
