West-Coast Voice

The course has taught you the east-coast model: a rich oscillator carved down by a resonant filter and shaped by an ADSR. This étude builds the opposite philosophy — the west-coast (Buchla) voice, where a simple oscillator is made complex by wavefolding and articulated by a low-pass gate. Brightness, loudness, and length become one gesture instead of three.

Start here
  • Install VCV Rack 2 (free) — this étude is built in modular. Hardware owners can use a Make Noise Strega or similar; the MicroFreak is a limited approximation (it has a wavefolder but no true LPG).
  • Run the listening below first — Subotnick and Ciani are the hypothesis for this voice.
  • Save the patch as LAB-024B-westcoast.vcv and a Live set LAB-024B to record into.
  • Have a normal subtractive patch (saw → resonant low-pass → ADSR) ready on another track for the final A/B.
  • Expected time: two sessions — one to build the folder + LPG voice, one to write the phrase, fault, and A/B.
The point

East coast (Moog/Buchla's rival tradition) starts rich and subtracts. West coast (Buchla) starts simple and adds complexity through nonlinear shaping, then articulates with a gate that ties brightness to loudness the way an acoustic instrument does — hit it harder and it is louder, brighter, and rings longer, all at once. This étude makes one control carry all the expression, which is the west-coast idea in one sentence.

1 · Listen first — the west-coast sound

Three listening sources define this voice. They are not "ambient texture" — they are demonstrations of folding and the low-pass gate doing the work a filter and ADSR would do on the east coast.

01 Morton Subotnick — Silver Apples of the Moon the Buchla, articulated

The first electronic work commissioned for a record. Built entirely on the Buchla 100 — no keyboard, no subtractive filter sweeps. The sounds are folded and gated, bubbling and percussive.

Listen for
  • Plucky, bongo-like tones that get brighter as they get louder — that is the low-pass gate, not an envelope on a filter.
  • Timbres that bloom in complexity at peaks and simplify at troughs — wavefolding tracking dynamics.
  • The near-total absence of the "filter sweep" gesture you associate with synths — west coast does not work that way.
After — verify

Find a single repeated pluck and notice that the loud ones are brighter and ring longer than the quiet ones — brightness, loudness, and length are one control. That fusion is the LPG signature you will build.

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

02 Suzanne Ciani — Buchla concerts / Buchla Concerts 1975 folding as the whole timbre

Ciani is the great living voice of the Buchla. Her live patches are sequences of folded tones articulated by gates — the expression lives in how hard each event hits the folder and the LPG.

Listen for
  • Tones that morph in harmonic content as they swell — the wavefolder adding harmonics as level rises.
  • Watery, vocal-like movement created without a resonant filter anywhere.
  • How a single sequence stays interesting because each step is folded/gated to a different depth.
After — verify

Try to locate a resonant filter "wah." You will not find one — the motion is folding depth changing, not a cutoff sweeping. That absence is the test of whether you are hearing west coast or east coast.

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

03 Suzanne Ciani — "The Fifth Wave" / any LPG-driven pluck piece the bongo / plucked decay

The vactrol low-pass gate gives a characteristic "bongo" decay — a soft, organic pluck that no ADSR quite matches because the brightness and the volume fall together along the vactrol's natural curve.

Listen for
  • Percussive events with a soft, rounded attack and a decay that darkens as it fades — not the sharp gate of a VCA.
  • The "rubbery" organic quality of the decay — that is the vactrol's slow response, modelled in VCV's LPG.
After — verify

Compare a pluck here to a synth pluck made with an ADSR closing a VCA: the LPG one gets darker as it decays; the ADSR one stays the same colour and just gets quieter. That darkening-on-decay is the LPG.

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

Write a one-sentence hypothesis contrasting the two philosophies. The target is something like — west coast makes a simple oscillator complex by folding (more level = more harmonics) and articulates it with a low-pass gate that ties brightness, loudness, and decay length into one gesture, where east coast carves a rich oscillator with a resonant filter and a separate ADSR. The build tests whether you can hear and make the difference.

3 · Concepts — east coast versus west coast

ConceptIn plain words
East-coast (subtractive)Start with a harmonically rich oscillator (saw/square), remove harmonics with a resonant low-pass filter, and shape volume with an ADSR envelope on a VCA. Brightness and loudness are separate controls. This is what the course has taught so far.
WavefoldingA nonlinear shaper that "folds" a waveform back on itself when it exceeds a threshold. A pure sine has no harmonics; fold it and you generate new harmonics — and the harder you drive the input (more level), the more folds and the brighter the tone. Complexity from amplitude, not from a filter.
Low-pass gate (LPG)A combined VCA + low-pass filter controlled together, classically through a vactrol (an LED + light-dependent resistor). Ping it with a trigger and it opens then closes along the vactrol's slow organic curve, so as the sound fades it also gets darker — louder = brighter + longer, all at once. This is what makes the "bongo/plucked" Buchla sound.
Vactrol curveThe vactrol does not respond instantly — the LDR lags the LED. That lag gives the LPG its characteristic soft attack and rubbery decay that a clean digital VCA cannot reproduce. VCV's LPG modules model this; it is the reason an LPG pluck never sounds like an ADSR pluck.

Step 01

Make a simple oscillator complex by folding

Do this
  1. In VCV, add a VCO (e.g. Fundamental VCO-1 or Befaco EvenVCO). Take its sine (or triangle) output — the harmonically poorest waveform on purpose.
  2. Add a wavefolder module. Reliable free choices in the VCV library: Bogaudio "FOLD", the wavefolder in Surge XT "Waveshaper", or a Befaco folder. Patch the VCO sine into the folder input.
  3. Turn the folder's fold/gain knob up slowly while a note sounds: a pure tone sprouts new harmonics, getting brighter and buzzier as you drive it harder. Confirm on a scope module that the smooth sine is now folded.
  4. Add an envelope generator (AD or function module like Bogaudio "FOLLOW"? use Befaco "Rampage" or Fundamental ADSR) and patch its output to the folder's fold-depth CV input (with attenuation). Now fold depth — and therefore brightness — tracks the envelope: dynamics drive timbre.

  • Why a sine, not a saw: if you fold a saw you cannot tell the folding from the source's own harmonics. Folding a sine isolates the lesson — every new harmonic you hear was made by the fold, controllable by level.

Step 02

Articulate with the LPG, not a filter

Do this
  1. Add a low-pass gate module — VCV has several. Recommended free choices: Bogaudio "LLPG" (a dedicated vactrol-modelled LPG), or Audible Instruments "Veils" used in its LPG mode. Patch the folder output into the LPG audio input; LPG output → audio interface module → Live.
  2. Add a trigger/clock source (e.g. Fundamental "SEQ-3" gates, or a clock + a manual button). Patch a trigger into the LPG's ping/excite input.
  3. Each trigger now produces a pluck: the LPG opens (bright + loud) and closes along the vactrol curve (darkening as it fades). Tune the LPG's decay/response so it reads as a soft bongo, not a hard click.
  4. Vary the trigger strength / velocity (or the level into the LPG): harder pings give brighter, louder, longer plucks — confirm this fusion by ear. That is the LPG behaving as VCA and VCF at once.

  • If your LPG has a VCA/LPG/both switch: use "both" (the combined mode). VCA-only gives a hard volume gate with no darkening; LPG (or "both") gives the brightness-tracks-loudness behaviour that defines the west-coast pluck.

Step C — a phrase where one control carries the expression

Step 03

One control, all the expression

  1. Choose the single expressive control: either fold depth (brightness blooming) or LPG ping strength (pluck dynamics). Drive it from one source — a slow LFO, a random/sample-and-hold, or a sequencer's velocity lane.
  2. Hold everything else still: fixed pitch (or a slow sequence), fixed clock. Let the chosen control modulate, and record a short generative phrase (2–8 bars) where the listener's interest comes from that one parameter moving.
  3. Document the patch: every module, every cable, and which control carries the expression. The phrase must fail to be interesting if you freeze that one control — prove it to yourself.
Why one control

The west-coast discipline is that a single nonlinear gesture (folding, or the gate's excitation) can carry a whole part — the expression is in the depth of one move, not in an arrangement of many parts. If freezing your chosen control leaves the phrase still interesting, the expression was actually coming from somewhere else, and the étude has not landed.

5 · The prescribed fault — induce and diagnose

Required

Replace the LPG with a resonant low-pass filter (VCF) plus an envelope on its cutoff — the east-coast articulator — and hear why it is not the west-coast voice. This is the single most common mistake when people first try to "do Buchla."

Fault

Using a resonant VCF instead of an LPG

  1. Patch the folder output through a resonant low-pass VCF with an envelope on its cutoff and a separate VCA/ADSR for volume — the classic east-coast pluck.
  2. Play the same phrase. Listen: the VCF version has a resonant "wah/zap" on each note, the brightness is set by cutoff (independent of loudness), and the decay does not darken the way the LPG's did. Crank resonance and it whistles — a sound the LPG never makes.
  3. Switch back to the LPG and A/B. Write the rule: the LPG fuses brightness + loudness + length on a vactrol curve; the VCF separates them and adds resonance. Same notes, fundamentally different instrument.

6 · The Folder/LPG Test — certified here

Certification

Tell west coast from east coast, by ear and by patch

  1. Play your LPG pluck and your VCF pluck back to back. State, in mechanism, the two differences: (a) LPG darkens on decay, VCF holds its colour; (b) LPG ties brightness to loudness, VCF sets them independently and adds resonance.
  2. Play your folded tone and a subtractive saw-through-filter tone. State where each one's harmonics come from: folding (amplitude-driven, from a sine) vs subtraction (a filter removing harmonics from a rich source).
  3. File one sentence naming which single control carries the expression in your phrase and what it does mechanically.
Pass condition

You can identify west-coast vs east-coast articulation blind, and explain the difference as mechanism (vactrol fusion, amplitude-driven harmonics) rather than vibe. If your only description is "it sounds more organic," the model has not landed.

7 · Pass gate — A/B against subtractive

Gate

The same line, west coast and east coast

  1. Take your generative phrase and remake the same line subtractively: saw → resonant low-pass → ADSR/VCA, matched in tempo and loudness.
  2. A/B the two at matched loudness. Name three things the folding + LPG version does that the subtractive version cannot (e.g. "brightness rises with loudness on every note," "decays darken," "the timbre blooms from a sine").
  3. Name one thing the subtractive version does better (e.g. "independent control of cutoff for a deliberate filter sweep").
Pass condition

A phrase whose timbre and dynamics are driven by wavefolding + an LPG, with the patch documented, A/B'd against the same line made subtractively — and a clear, mechanical account of what folding + LPG gained and what it lost.

Worked system diff — the expected granularity

"I thought 'plucky synth' meant an ADSR on a filter; the Buchla pluck is a low-pass gate whose brightness and length fall together — louder is brighter is longer, all from one ping."

Étude principle

West coast adds complexity by folding and articulates by gating — brightness, loudness, and length become a single gesture, where east coast keeps them three separate knobs.
