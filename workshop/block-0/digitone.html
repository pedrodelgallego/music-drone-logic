Adding the Digitone II

The Elektron Digitone II is a polyphonic FM synthesizer and sequencer. It makes chords, pads, bass lines, bells, and metallic percussion — all the melodic and harmonic material the Digitakt II does not cover. This page explains its role, and works through the one real problem it introduces: a stereo audio signal that needs to get into a 4-input interface that is already nearly full.

Optional · New instrument · Elektron Digitone II · MOTU M4 · Overbridge · Digitakt II

What you are adding

The Digitone II (Elektron, 2024) is a polyphonic FM synthesizer — up to 16 voices, stereo output, on-board filter, overdrive, and effects. It has its own deep step sequencer — 16 tracks, p-locks, conditional trigs, micro-timing, per-track lengths, song mode. That sequencer is the same as the Digitakt II's, so if you have learned one, you already know the other. MIDI travels over 3.5 mm TRS (adapters included) and USB-C. Overbridge lets you stream every track as a separate audio channel into Live over USB.

Its role in the rig

Every box should have one job. Adding the Digitone II turns a two-instrument rig into a three-instrument rig where the jobs divide cleanly.

BoxIts one job in this rig
Elektron Digitakt IIDrums, percussion, sampling, and the rhythmic backbone. It sequences itself, sequences the MicroFreak over MIDI, and can pull audio from anything into its sampler.
Elektron Digitone IIMelody, harmony, pads, FM bass, and inharmonic textures. Polyphonic — it can hold a chord. Works as the melodic brain of a track.
Arturia MicroFreakOne carefully chosen voice. Mono, expressive, weird. Still the single-line counterpoint or lead against what the Digitone holds.
The sequencer carries over

If you have spent time on the Digitakt II's sequencer — p-locks (settings saved per step), conditional trigs (steps that only fire sometimes), micro-timing, trig conditions — all of that works exactly the same on the Digitone II. The two boxes share the same sequencer engine. Learning one taught you both.

Where does its stereo audio go? The M4 constraint

This is the real question. The MOTU M4 has four inputs. They are already assigned:

M4 inputs — current rig before adding the Digitone II
Input 1MicroFreak audio out · mono · ¼″ TS
Input 2Free — one mono jack available
Input 3Digitakt II main out L · ¼″ TRS
Input 4Digitakt II main out R · ¼″ TRS

One input is free: input 2. But the Digitone II is a stereo instrument. A stereo signal needs two inputs. You cannot plug a stereo synth into one mono jack without losing half the signal. So you have three real options.

Recommendation

Use Overbridge by default. One USB-C cable, no M4 inputs consumed, separate stems in Live, full recall. Install Overbridge once and leave it. Switch to the through-the-Digitakt route on any session where you want to resample the Digitone II directly into the Digitakt sampler — it is the fastest way to capture FM textures into your drum patterns. Only consider analog-into-the-M4 after you have upgraded to an interface with more inputs.

Routing diagrams

Option 1 — Overbridge path (default)

The Digitone II's audio travels over USB directly into Live as a plug-in. The M4 handles everything else. The dashed box shows that the Digitone II is present in Live's mixer without using any M4 input.

Option 2 — Through the Digitakt II (jam / resample)

The Digitone II's audio goes into the Digitakt II's hardware inputs. The Digitakt sums it with its own sounds and sends one stereo pair to the M4. You can resample the Digitone II directly inside the Digitakt.

Digitone II L/R out → ¼″ TS ×2 → Digitakt II in A / B → summed stereo → Digitakt II main out L/R → M4 IN 3/4 → Live
  • Resampling bonus: with the Digitone II going into the Digitakt's audio inputs, the Digitakt can record it — one button. You can capture an FM chord, chop it up as a sample, and play it back on a drum track. This is one of the fastest ways to turn pitched material into rhythmic texture.
  • Trade-off: in this path, the Digitone II's audio passes through the Digitakt's converter stage. It is fine quality, but the two instruments are no longer separate channels in Live — they share one stereo pair. You lose per-instrument mixing in Live and you lose Overbridge recall for the Digitone II.
  • Switching between options: you do not have to choose forever. Keep the Overbridge cable connected. When you want to resample, plug the two ¼″ cables from the Digitone II into the Digitakt inputs and route the Digitakt's input monitoring on. When you are done resampling, unplug and go back to Overbridge.

Two-Elektron workflow

With both Elektron boxes running, the simplest division of labour is also the most musical one.

Digitakt IIDigitone II
Kicks, snares, claps, hats — the rhythmic skeletonPads, chords, FM bass lines, bells — the harmonic body
Samples — field recordings, sliced loops, resampled texturesMelodic leads, evolving textures via p-locked FM parameters
The clock brain in hardware-led configs (C and D)Follows the Digitakt's clock, or follows Live's clock alongside it
Sequences the MicroFreak over MIDI (as a third voice)Can also sequence the MicroFreak — or be sequenced by Live directly

Clock and MIDI between the two boxes

  • Live stays master in the default (Config A/C). Live sends MIDI clock over USB to both the Digitakt II and the Digitone II independently. Both follow. Neither sends clock to the other — Live is the single leader. Enable Clock Receive and Transport Receive in SETTINGS → MIDI Config → Sync on both boxes.
  • In screen-free jams (Config B/C hardware-led): the Digitakt II becomes the clock master. Its MIDI out connects to the Digitone II's MIDI in. The Digitone II then follows the Digitakt's tempo and transport. On the Digitakt, enable Clock Send and Transport Send; on the Digitone II, enable Clock Receive and Transport Receive.
  • MIDI notes between the boxes: you can sequence the Digitone II's synth tracks from the Digitakt's MIDI tracks — select a MIDI track on the Digitakt, set its MIDI channel to match the Digitone II track you want to trigger, and the Digitakt's step sequencer drives the Digitone II's voices. This lets you build rhythmic patterns on the Digitakt that fire FM notes on the Digitone II.
  • Do not let both boxes send clock. The same rule as always: one leader. If both are set to send clock, you get confusion and timing drift.

Where this fits in the "four configs"

The four configurations described on Block 0 index extend naturally with the Digitone II added. The audio routing choice (Overbridge or through-Digitakt) combines with the clock choice independently.

Config A/C + Overbridge

  • Live is the clock master over USB.
  • Digitone II audio enters Live via Overbridge — no M4 inputs used.
  • Full separate stems, total recall, most CPU.
  • Best for: producing, arranging, all the labs.

Config B/C + through-Digitakt

  • Digitakt II leads the clock over DIN or USB.
  • Digitone II audio into Digitakt inputs A/B — one combined stereo pair out.
  • No computer overhead, instant resampling, screen-optional.
  • Best for: flow sessions, ideas, live jamming.
