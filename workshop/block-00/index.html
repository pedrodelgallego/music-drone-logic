Workshop/Block 00 — Foundations
Block 00 — Foundations · optional

The atoms — learn one control at a time, before you build systems

The rest of the workshop teaches you to use sound: drones, techno, sampling, generative systems. It assumes you can already make and shape a sound. This block is the layer underneath — the building blocks, taught one at a time, the way Block 1 teaches the studio. Already fluent? Skip it. New, or shaky on the basics? Do the ones that match your weak spots first.

Optional · Fundamentals · anchored to gear you own — MicroFreak · Digitakt II · Digitone II · Launchpad · Ableton Live 12 Suite

Why this exists

Block 1 teaches the atoms of engineering — gain, signal path, monitoring — one at a time, and it sticks. Every other skill deserves the same treatment. Each page here isolates one set of building blocks, drills them on a specific instrument you own, and ends in a single "prove it" task.

  • How each page works. One anchor instrument, the atoms taught one control at a time, free practice tools, and a single proof gate at the end. No theory dumps. You play, you hear, you keep.
  • Where it sits. Below Block 0 — The Rig (setup) and before Block 1 — The Bench. A beginner does Foundations → Rig → Bench → the blocks. Everyone else cherry-picks.

Suggested order

If you do them all: Synthesis first (it underlies everything), then Rhythm, then Harmony (your incoming Digitone II makes it daily), then Mixing — Dynamics (sidechain is the techno move), then EQ, Effects, Ear Training, and Arrangement last. But the table above is the real guide — start where you're weakest.

What "done" looks like

Every primer ends in one proof — not a quiz, a thing you make
  • Synthesis — build the recipe cookbook from blank patches, and recreate two sounds by ear
  • Rhythm — program and count eight grooves out loud
  • Harmony — voice one progression three ways
  • Dynamics — a controlled mix and one audible sidechain pump, with your settings defended
  • EQ — fix a muddy and a harsh sound with named moves, checked on the analyzer
  • Effects — recreate three named effect sounds from an init effect
  • Ear training — match five target sounds without looking
