Experimental Music Workshop

Learning Experimental Music: A Systems Approach

A multi-year workshop in experimental electronic music, taught as systems thinking and sound engineering. 35 build labs, 8 replication labs, 8 listening programmes, and 7 capstones across three years — grown from Lab Note 001.

North star

The graduate can walk into an unfamiliar setup, draw the audio path, identify the control path, calibrate levels, build a musical system, diagnose failure, print the result, and explain what happened.

The operating loop

Every track lab, bench lab, and capstone runs the same loop. The loop is the pedagogy; techniques are the occasions for running it.

Comparative listening→ System hypothesis→ Draw the path→ Calibrate→ Build→ Inject a fault→ Diagnose→ Print→ Explain the delta

Three strands

Build labs · 001–035 + R1–R8

  • Track labs — a full original piece around one technique
  • Études — short focused exercises
  • Bench labs — engineering: calibration, routing, monitoring, the rig
  • Replication labs — a reference rebuilt from your own analysis; copy to learn, compose your own

Listening programme · L1–L8

  • One programme per block: records, samples, video sessions
  • Works analyzed as systems — six questions, ending in the likely audio path
  • One stolen principle per block: a structure, never material

Capstones · C1–C7

  • Gates close a block with a constraints-only brief
  • Year capstones add a reprise of older work and a year synthesis
  • The final capstone is a published release and portfolio defense
