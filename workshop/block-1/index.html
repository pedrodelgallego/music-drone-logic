Block 1 — The Bench

Listen, calibrate the studio, then prove it with a piece

OverviewListeningLabsRebuildCapstoneRep lessonsDashboard
Engineering focus

By the end (project C1) you can draw your whole studio's signal path and point to every place the volume can change. You can tell apart clipping at the input, the track, the bus, and the master, and say exactly what you're listening through. And you can record the same sound cleanly at three volumes, then explain which one you'd keep.

  • New to synthesis? Do Block 00 — Foundations first. It teaches the atoms — oscillators, filters, envelopes, LFOs — one control at a time on your own gear, and ends in a sound-recipe cookbook. This block assumes you can already make and shape a sound.
  • Optional setup first: Block 0 — The Rig is a deep, opinionated guide to wiring and configuring your exact gear, with diagrams, side-by-side setup comparisons, and a Day 1 → Year 1 plan. You don't need it — the labs below walk you through the minimum wiring — but it's there when you want the why.

Required path

Do these in this order
  1. Programme L1 — do the three listening sessions and name one idea you'll borrow
  2. Lab 001 — map and test your signal path and every volume control
  3. Lab 002 — test your timing, clock, and monitoring
  4. R1 — rebuild the eight bars from L1 Session 2 and fix your worksheet
  5. Required drills — grid + clock, per-step settings + mutes, sends + live mixing — the three skills Lab 003 expects you to have
  6. Lab 003 — build the worked-through hypnotic techno piece
  7. Lab 004 — build a short piece from a single sound
  8. Lab 004b — build a beatless version from the same sound, and update your volume notes for long, held sounds
  9. Capstone C1 — make a second piece from a set of rules, and re-test your studio setup
  • Rebuild lessons are optional practice. Use one when a track shows up a specific weak spot in your listening or studio skills. The exceptions are the three drills Lab 003 lists as prerequisites, plus R1 — those are required.

Listening programme L1 — repetition & hypnosis

Session 1 · Records

Four engines of repetition

The same idea — a loop that never gets boring — done four different ways. The fourth is the extreme case: repetition slowed all the way to a standstill. Analyse each one with the six questions, then say what makes the four approaches different.

Basic Channel — “Quadrant Dub I”dub-filtered loop
Plastikman — Consumed (title track)subtractive automation
Donato Dozzy & Nuel — The Aquaplano Sessionsmodulation drift
Coil — Time Machinesthe zero-rate loop
Listen for

What actually changes over eight minutes? What never changes? Which machine or hand is making the change?

Session 2 · Samples

Deconstruction — one track to its sources

Pick one hypnotic track and work backwards through it: kick, hats, chord stab. For each one, ask what the raw sound was, what was done to it, and in what order. The sixth question matters most: what setup had to exist for this record to be made?

Session 3 · Video

A hardware-techno live set, watched for restraint

Watch the hands, not the lights. Where are they? What is being adjusted live, and what was set up in advance? What is the performer listening to? What could go wrong, and what couldn't be fixed afterwards?

Minimum pass gate

Track labs & capstones — before any quality judgment
  • There is sound you can hear
  • A wiring diagram exists — drawn before you patch, fixed afterwards
  • You found and fixed one fault: what went wrong, why, the fix, and how to avoid it next time
  • A record of what you saved — which parts are audio, which are settings, and what's gone for good
  • One sentence on what surprised you: “I thought it worked like X; it actually worked like Y”
