Block 1 · Replication lesson

Replicate: Economy with Teeth

Copy one clear section of Surgeon — “Magneze”. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: one synth line into distortion and filter, ridden by hand - the loop’s aggression curve is the arrangement. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

The system you are replicating
Source recordSurgeon — “Magneze” (1995, Downwards — the debut Surgeon EP)
The effectone modulated voice rides the whole track through escalating drive — Birmingham techno’s violence at deep-techno economy; the single element feels bigger every bar with nothing added
The mechanismone synth line into distortion and filter, ridden by hand — the loop’s aggression curve is the arrangement

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

Before you start
  • Gear: MicroFreak → M4 in 1, DT 990 Pro in the phones jack — the lab-001 wiring; the Digitakt II rests this lesson
  • New set saved as REP-MAGNEZE, tempo 132 — then warp REF and adjust by ear
  • Distortion is the safety hazard of this lesson: drive compresses and piles on high harmonics, so it gets dangerous before it gets loud. Phones at the lab-001 mark, never higher; every new drive setting is auditioned once at the set level and gain-compensated before it loops
  • What you will make: a short replica of Surgeon — “Magneze”. Focus on this effect: one modulated voice rides the whole track through escalating drive - Birmingham techno’s violence at deep-techno economy.
  • What you will turn off: extra parts, decoration, and shortcuts that hide whether the mechanism works.
  • What you will prove: your version and REF use the same core mechanism: one synth line into distortion and filter, ridden by hand - the loop’s aggression curve is the arrangement.
  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Surgeon — “Magneze”. Choose the clearest short section. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. Count the elements. How many are doing the work?
    Answer

    Very few - drums and one modulated voice that owns the foreground for the whole track. Birmingham’s reputation is violence, but the parts list is deep-techno sparse.

  2. What gets added as the track builds?
    Answer

    Nothing. The escalation is the same element getting fiercer - no new line, no new layer arrives to mark the build. Relisten across two distant minutes and try to name one genuinely new element; you will name a new intensity instead.

  3. What, physically, is “fiercer”?
    Answer

    Harmonics. Drive density and a filter opening - the voice’s spectrum fills upward and sideways while its notes stay put. Aggression here is a measurable thing: more partials, more grit per hit.

  4. Is minute three louder than minute one?
    Answer

    Barely, if at all - the violence is spectral, not metered. A meter across both minutes would shrug; your chest would not. That split between level and ferocity is the entire replication.

  5. Where is the arrangement?
    Answer

    In the ride. The curve of aggression - climbing, held, eased, slammed - is the form, and sections are positions on that curve rather than changes of material.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Digitakt II: [TRK] + [TRIG 1-16] selects the active track. Press [RECORD] for grid recording, press the numbered [TRIG] keys to place or remove hits, and press [PLAY] to hear the pattern.
  • Digitakt II timing: press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to set swing. 50% is no swing. In grid recording, hold a trig and press [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to check or zero micro-timing.
  • Digitakt II sound and sync: press [SRC] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to choose a sample. Press [FX] to reach delay/reverb/chorus sends. Press [SETTINGS] for MIDI Config → Sync; use [YES] or [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to toggle receive settings.
  • MicroFreak: select the oscillator engine with the Type control, then set cutoff, resonance, envelope, LFO, and volume with the front-panel knobs before recording. When the page gives a value, move that knob slowly while A/Bing against REF.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
  • Sound target: a solo-flip between REF and your take lands on the same trajectory — one line whose violence grows while its loudness stands still, at matched loudness throughout.
  • One voice: a single oscillator through the MicroFreak’s filter, one drive stage after it — nothing else pitched.
  • Only two controls move for the whole take: drive amount and filter cutoff; the Utility behind the drive pins the loudness.
  • Nothing enters and nothing leaves — the curve is the form.

The track’s machinery in one row: one looped MicroFreak line, one Saturator with a Utility behind it for gain compensation, and a three-to-four-minute two-knob ride — drive up, output down, cutoff carving — recorded as automation.

MicroFreak · one osc · one filter · looped line → M4 in 1 · LINE → Saturator · drive ridden → Utility · loudness pinned ≈ −12 dBFS → 3–4 min ride · automation

The same loop at three minutes’ distance. The bars are the voice’s spectrum — harmonics multiplying as the drive rises — and the dashed line is the loudness, pinned by the Utility. Everything grows except the level.

Phase 1 · ≈ 10 min

The reference and the track

Phase focus: the reference and the track. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live ⌘ T, rename the track REF (⌘ R), and drag “Magneze” into it.
  2. Live Find two or three minutes where the central line audibly escalates, and loop them.
  3. Live Drop Audio Effects → Utility on REF and trim so the section peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.
  4. Live REF stays one solo-press away all lesson.
  5. Live ⌘ T, rename LINE, Ext. In 1, fader at 0 dB, armed.
  6. Logbook Sketch the section’s aggression curve in the logbook as one pencil line — rises, shelves, the slam.
  7. Logbook Phase 4 performs this drawing.
Expected result

The record loops at ≈ −12 dBFS, one named and routed track waits for the line, and the curve is on paper.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
REF later feels fiercer in a way yours can never reachUtility never trimmed — ferocity and level conflatedTrim until the looped section peaks ≈ −12 dBFS, same as your line
No meter movement when testing the inputExt. In channel doesn’t match the physical jackMicroFreak on 1 — the lab-001 wiring table
Phase 2 · ≈ 15 min

The voice and the line — raw, dry, insistent

Phase focus: the voice and the line — raw, dry, insistent. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. MicroFreak MicroFreak: Type → V.Analog, Paraphonic OFF, LP filter, Cutoff ≈ 40 %, Resonance ≈ 20 %.
  2. MicroFreak Envelope: attack 0, decay medium, sustain low.
  3. MicroFreak + Live MIDI track, MIDI To → MicroFreak, channel 1: transcribe the line’s rhythm and register from REF into a one-bar clip.
  4. MicroFreak + Live Under that much drive exact pitches are negotiable — match the register and the insistence;
  5. MicroFreak + Live where a pitch is buried, choose the nearest one that keeps the menace.
  6. Live Loop the clip and set the M4 input-1 gain on the looping line, peaks ≈ −12 dBFS — this dry level is the headroom the drive stage will spend.
  7. Live A/B the dry loop against REF: yours should sound like the record’s line before the violence — same bones, no teeth yet.
Expected result

A dry one-bar loop in the record’s register, dull on top by design, printing at ≈ −12 dBFS — material with somewhere to grow.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The dry line already sounds aggressiveCutoff too open, resonance too high — the growth spent in advanceClose the filter toward 40 % and back the resonance off; the drive stage owns the brightness
MIDI notes do nothingTrack output not set to the MicroFreak portMIDI To → MicroFreak, channel 1 — check local keys vs USB
Phase 3 · ≈ 15 min

The drive stage, calibrated against itself

Phase focus: the drive stage, calibrated against itself. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live On LINE: Audio Effects → Saturator (curve Analog Clip), then Utility directly after it.
  2. Live This pair is the whole transformation chain.
  3. Live Calibrate CLEAN: Drive 0 dB, Utility 0 dB — confirm the loop still peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.
  4. Live Calibrate MEAN: Drive ≈ +12 dB, then pull the Utility down (≈ −10 dB, trust the meter over the number) until the loop peaks ≈ −12 dBFS again.
  5. Live Calibrate SAVAGE: Drive ≈ +20 dB, Utility down (≈ −16 dB) to the same ≈ −12 dBFS.
  6. Live Audition each state once at the set level before looping it.
  7. Live + Logbook Write all three Drive/Utility pairs in the logbook.
  8. Live + Logbook Flip between the three states at matched loudness and say out loud what changes: density and harmonics, not volume.
  9. Live + Logbook That sentence is the record’s engine.
Expected result

Three calibrated violence settings that meter identically — a knob’s worth of ferocity with the loudness lie subtracted, and the pairs on paper for phase 4.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The driven state always sounds “better”, instantlyIt is just louder — compensation not finishedTrim the Utility until the meters match before judging; the preference that survives is the real one
The drive sounds flat and fizzy, not muscularClipping at the converter instead of in the pluginKeep the M4 gain at the ledger setting — the violence belongs inside Live, after a clean print level
Phase 4 · ≈ 25 min

Perform the ride — two knobs, one curve

Phase focus: perform the ride — two knobs, one curve. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Translate the pencil curve into plain words with timestamps: “climb to MEAN by 1:00 · shelf · ease at 2:00 · slam to SAVAGE at 2:30 · hold to the end.”
  2. Live + Logbook ⌘ M, MIDI-map Drive and the Utility’s gain to two encoders — or pre-draw the Utility’s compensation from the logbook pairs and ride Drive alone against it.
  3. MicroFreak + Live Arm LINE and record 3–4 minutes in Arrangement: left hand on Drive (with its mirror), right hand on the MicroFreak’s cutoff carving the top end.
  4. MicroFreak + Live Automation recording on, no other control touched.
  5. MicroFreak + Live Play it end to end at the mark.
  6. MicroFreak + Live The loudness must sit still the whole way — if a passage swells, the mirror lagged;
  7. MicroFreak + Live redo that passage from the written pairs.
  8. Live A/B against REF at matched loudness, then export: File → Export Audio/Video, WAV, named REP-MAGNEZE.
Expected result

Three to four minutes where one line grows from filed-off to feral along a planned curve, the meter reading the same at both ends — escalation made entirely of density.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
Loudness creeps up with the driveThe Utility mirror lagged the rideRe-draw the compensation from the logbook pairs and re-record the passage — the lie comes back the moment the mirror slips
The ride wanders instead of buildingNo plan — the hand followed the mood of the momentPerform the paper curve, not your mood; if the curve is wrong, fix it on paper first and ride again
Verify against the record

Solo-flip between REF and your export at matched loudness — phones at the mark, no level riding while you judge.

  • At matched loudness, what changed between your minute one and minute three?
    Answer

    Spectral density and harmonics — not level, and not new elements. Loop a bar from each minute back to back: same notes, same meter reading, different animal. If the later bar is mostly just louder, the Utility lagged the Saturator — recalibrate and re-ride. Relisten to the record’s own first and third minutes the same way and hear the identical trade.

  • Could you draw the ride from memory?
    Answer

    You should be able to — one pencil line with rises, shelves, and one big push. A composed ride survives as a drawing; a wandered one survives as a shrug. Relisten to your print with the pencil out, draw what you hear, and compare it against the phase-1 plan; where the two lines disagree is where the hand drifted.

  • Logbook — three-line miss list. Matched: where your version produces the record’s effect. Missed: where it doesn’t — and your best guess why. Change next: the one move you’d try first.
  • These three lines feed the full miss list in replication lab R1.
  • Carries into the lab: a second voice is one answer to monotony; a fiercer version of the first voice is another — when Lab 004’s three minutes sag, this record is the rescue made of drive and cutoff instead of new material.
