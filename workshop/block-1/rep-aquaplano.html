Block 1 · Replication lesson

Replicate: drift you can’t point to

Copy one clear section of Donato Dozzy & Nuel — The Aquaplano Sessions. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: Slow, unsynced modulators inside the voice: they never line up with the bars, so the timbre never lands in the same place twice. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

The system you are replicating
Source recordDonato Dozzy & Nuel — The Aquaplano Sessions
The effectA loop that breathes for minutes with no audible hand on it — no two passes identical
The mechanismSlow, unsynced modulators inside the voice: they never line up with the bars, so the timbre never lands in the same place twice

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

Before you start
  • MicroFreak on M4 in 1, recording armed path tested in Live
  • Gain set by the lab 001 ledger — a quiet patch tempts you to over-gain
  • What you will make: a short replica of Donato Dozzy & Nuel — The Aquaplano Sessions. Focus on this effect: A loop that breathes for minutes with no audible hand on it - no two passes identical.
  • What you will turn off: extra parts, decoration, and shortcuts that hide whether the mechanism works.
  • What you will prove: your version and REF use the same core mechanism: Slow, unsynced modulators inside the voice: they never line up with the bars, so the timbre never lands in the same place twice.
  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Donato Dozzy & Nuel — The Aquaplano Sessions. Choose the clearest short section. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. Pick one sound and follow it for the minute — what about it changed (brightness? distance? roughness?)?
    Answer

    Brightness, mostly - the tone breathes between dull and slightly open - and with it a sense of distance, as if the sound drifts nearer and farther without moving.

  2. Could you point to the MOMENT anything changed?
    Answer

    No - and that is the test of the mechanism. The change is continuous and slower than attention, so by the time you notice a difference it has already been happening for many bars.

  3. Does the drift ever line up with the bars, or does it float free?
    Answer

    It floats free. Listen across several loop cycles: the brightest point lands somewhere different against the bar every time.

  4. Is anyone’s hand audible — or does it feel like weather?
    Answer

    Weather. There is no gesture, no intention, no moment of decision anywhere in the minute - the variation was built into the patch and then left alone.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

  • Sound target: a tone in the section’s register and color, drifting at the record’s own rate.
  • No edges: no nameable moment of change — in yours or theirs.
  • Free-floating: the drift floats free of the bar grid — unsynced, like theirs.
  • Hands: zero, after pressing record.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Digitakt II: [TRK] + [TRIG 1-16] selects the active track. Press [RECORD] for grid recording, press the numbered [TRIG] keys to place or remove hits, and press [PLAY] to hear the pattern.
  • Digitakt II timing: press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to set swing. 50% is no swing. In grid recording, hold a trig and press [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to check or zero micro-timing.
  • Digitakt II sound and sync: press [SRC] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to choose a sample. Press [FX] to reach delay/reverb/chorus sends. Press [SETTINGS] for MIDI Config → Sync; use [YES] or [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to toggle receive settings.
  • MicroFreak: select the oscillator engine with the Type control, then set cutoff, resonance, envelope, LFO, and volume with the front-panel knobs before recording. When the page gives a value, move that knob slowly while A/Bing against REF.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
Phase 1 — ~25 minutes

Match the tone before anything moves

Phase focus: match the tone before anything moves. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live Import the record on a REF track. Drop your chosen track from The Aquaplano Sessions onto a dedicated REF track in Live and loop its most characteristic stretch — a 2–3 minute stretch is fine here, the drift is slow.
  2. Live Gain-match it with a Utility so comparisons are honest, and keep it one solo away for the whole session.
  3. MicroFreak Match the patch’s tone. On the MicroFreak, build the section’s central sound: pick the engine that gets nearest, match the pitch and register by ear, set the filter where theirs sits.
  4. Live A/B against the REF — yours, theirs, yours — until the gap is the hardware’s, not your ears’.
Expected result

A static tone that sits next to the record’s central sound as a plausible sibling — same register, same weight, same filter colour — and does not move at all yet.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
Every engine sounds wrongChasing the record’s space instead of its tone — their sound carries room and tape you do not have yetMatch the dry core only; accept that the air around it is theirs, not the patch’s
The tone matches at one note but not in contextWrong register — the same patch an octave off reads as a different instrumentRe-check pitch and octave against the REF before touching anything else
Phase 2 — ~25 minutes

Wire the two movers

Phase focus: wire the two movers. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Two movers slower than attention.

Do this
  1. MicroFreak Route the first mover. In the mod matrix, route LFO → Cutoff at a SMALL amount.
  2. MicroFreak Small means you barely hear it on one pass — listen to how faint the record’s movement is and start there.
  3. MicroFreak Slow the LFO to the record’s rate. Set the LFO very slow and UNSYNCED — free-running.
  4. Live Solo the REF and time their drift: how long does one breath of brightness take?
  5. Live Match it.
  6. MicroFreak A synced LFO repeats with the loop and the spell collapses — theirs never lines up with the bars, so neither should yours.
  7. MicroFreak Add the second mover at a different speed. Route the cycling envelope → Wave or Timbre, again at a small amount, with rise and fall times that give it a clearly different period from the LFO.
  8. MicroFreak Set its depth so the secondary movement is as faint as the record’s.
Expected result

On a single pass of the loop, almost nothing audible. Over a minute, an unmistakable slow breathing that never lands in the same place against the bar twice.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The movement is an obvious wobbleModulation amount too big — it reads as an effect, not weatherHalve the amount, then halve it again; the record’s movement is barely there on any one pass
The drift repeats in step with the loopThe LFO is synced to tempoSet it free-running (unsynced) and re-time it against the REF’s breath
Phase 3 — ~30 minutes

Set it running, then record with your hands off

Phase focus: set it running, then record with your hands off. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

MicroFreak (patch running) → TS → M4 in 1 → USB-C → Live track (record armed) → master → phones
Do this
  1. Digitakt II + Live Set it running and A/B the drift. Sequence a pattern matching the section’s figure — or hold the tone the way they do — and let it run next to the REF.
  2. Live A/B a minute of each: does yours breathe at their rate, and as deep?
  3. Live Adjust speed and amount until it does, then hands off.
  4. Live Record 3 minutes into Live without touching anything. Hands off is the discipline.
  5. Logbook If you hear something drifting away from the record, write it down instead of reaching for it.
Expected result

Three recorded minutes with zero performed events — and a tone that, A/B’d in any 10-second window, drifts at the record’s rate and depth.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
Yours breathes noticeably faster than theirsThe LFO rate was set by impatience, not by the REF — their breath can take half a minuteRe-time one full breath of the record with a watch, then slow the LFO until yours matches
You touched it mid-takeA drift away from the record felt like an error to fixDelete the take and go again — the note you should have written is the data, not the tweak
Phase 4 — ~15 minutes

Listen back, then look at the waveform

Phase focus: listen back, then look at the waveform. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Listen back twice. Once as an audience member, start to finish.
  2. Live Then once more A/Bing against the REF, following only the timbre — where is yours bright while theirs is dull, and does either ever announce the moment it changed?
  3. Live Observe — see it, not just hear it. Zoom the waveform at minute 0 and minute 2½ and LOOK at two cycles of the loop side by side — visibly different shapes from the same notes.
  4. Live The patch did that, not you — the same engine the record left running.
Expected result

Two waveform snapshots of the same bar that do not match — and no moment in either listening pass where you could say “there, it changed.”

Verify against the record

A/B your recording against the looped section — 10 seconds of yours, 10 of theirs — would a friend hesitate? Then answer two questions in writing.

Over a full minute of each, does yours breathe at their rate and depth, or does one of you drift faster?

Answer

Time it rather than feel it: one breath of brightness on the record can take twenty or thirty seconds, far slower than a first patch usually dares. If yours is faster, the LFO rate is the miss; if yours is deeper — a wobble where theirs is a haze — the amount is. Almost every first attempt errs fast and deep, because faint-and-slow feels like nothing is happening while you build it.

Can you point to the exact moment anything changed, in either take? (You must not be able to.)

Answer

In the record, no — and that is the pass condition for yours too. If you can name a moment in your take, something moved faster than attention: a mover too quick, an amount too large, or a hand that touched the patch mid-take. The drift should be a fact you can only verify by comparing distant minutes, never an event you can catch happening.

  • Logbook — the miss list, three lines: “Matched: …” · “Missed: … (best guess why)” · “Change next: …”
  • These three lines feed the full miss list in replication lab R1.
  • And one more line — the two mover speeds, and how long the sound took to feel alive. This is engine option three for lab 003, and the patch is a head start on étude 004’s “make it move by itself.”
