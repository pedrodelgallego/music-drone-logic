Block 1 · Replication lesson

Replicate: same loop, different light

Copy one clear section of Oscar Mulero — Black Propaganda. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: a static pattern; the motion is broadband - slow EQ tilt, room amount, noise level - global treatments riding over an unchanging engine. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

The system you are replicating
Source recordOscar Mulero — Black Propaganda (Warm Up, 2012)
The effectgrey-scale hypnotic techno where the same pattern persists while the spectrum slowly tilts — a fixed scene under changing light, developing though no notes change
The mechanisma static pattern; the motion is broadband — slow EQ tilt, room amount, noise level — global treatments riding over an unchanging engine

Listen: YouTube · Spotify

Before you start
  • Gear: Digitakt II main L/R → M4 in 3–4, the lab-003 wiring — the loop lives on the Digitakt II, the treatments live in Live; the MicroFreak contributes at most a printed noise tone
  • New set saved as REP-BLACKPROP · tempo near the record’s — write it down; save the Digitakt II pattern to its own slot
  • Phones at the lab-001 mark — a tilt toward the highs adds presence without adding meter level, the classic invitation to creep louder; re-check the mark after every tilt-heavy passage
  • What you will make: a short replica of Oscar Mulero — Black Propaganda. Focus on this effect: grey-scale hypnotic techno where the same pattern persists while the spectrum slowly tilts.
  • What you will turn off: extra parts, decoration, and shortcuts that hide whether the mechanism works.
  • What you will prove: your version and REF use the same core mechanism: a static pattern; the motion is broadband - slow EQ tilt, room amount, noise level - global treatments riding over an unchanging engine.
  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Oscar Mulero — Black Propaganda. Choose the clearest short section. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. Do the notes ever change — follow the pattern, not the sound?
    Answer

    Effectively no. Track the kick placement, the perc figure, the stab rhythm - they persist for minutes at a stretch. What you remember as “the track developing” was the same events wearing different light.

  2. What actually moves, then?
    Answer

    Broadband things: the spectral weight slides between dark and present, the room around the loop grows and dries up, a bed of noise rises and recedes.

  3. What color is this record?
    Answer

    Grey - and on purpose. Melody, chord color, and incident are all withheld, so the lighting is the only variable left, and the ear gives it full attention.

  4. Where does the form live?
    Answer

    In the treatments’ arc. The piece has a shape - it opens darker, gains presence and air, recedes - but that shape is drawn entirely in tilt, room, and noise over an unchanging engine.

  5. Could minute one and minute four swap places under a DJ’s hands?
    Answer

    Rhythmically, yes - the engine is interchangeable end to end, which is exactly what makes the track usable as a tool.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Digitakt II: [TRK] + [TRIG 1-16] selects the active track. Press [RECORD] for grid recording, press the numbered [TRIG] keys to place or remove hits, and press [PLAY] to hear the pattern.
  • Digitakt II timing: press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to set swing. 50% is no swing. In grid recording, hold a trig and press [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to check or zero micro-timing.
  • Digitakt II sound and sync: press [SRC] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to choose a sample. Press [FX] to reach delay/reverb/chorus sends. Press [SETTINGS] for MIDI Config → Sync; use [YES] or [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to toggle receive settings.
  • MicroFreak: select the oscillator engine with the Type control, then set cutoff, resonance, envelope, LFO, and volume with the front-panel knobs before recording. When the page gives a value, move that knob slowly while A/Bing against REF.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
  • Sound target: a solo-flip between REF and yours lands on the same grey persistence — an unchanging pattern under slowly shifting light, at matched loudness.
  • Pattern edits are forbidden after the loop is frozen — no new trigs, no p-locks, no mutes.
  • Exactly three controls for the whole arrangement: one EQ tilt macro, one send amount, one noise-track fader.
  • Every move is slow and global — if a change seems to belong to one element, it was the wrong kind of move.

The piece’s machinery in one row: a two-bar loop frozen on the Digitakt II, three global treatments mapped in Live — a spectral tilt, a room send, a noise fader — and a four-minute ride of those three controls printed as the arrangement.

static 2-bar loop · Digitakt II · frozen → EQ Eight tilt · one macro → room send · one amount → noise track · one fader → 4-minute ride · printed

The same scene three times. The blocks — the pattern — are identical in every panel; only the light line above them tilts, from weight in the lows to presence in the highs and back toward dark. The move from the first panel to the third takes the whole piece, and no block ever changes.

Phase 1 · ≈ 15 min

The reference and the grey engine

Phase focus: the reference and the grey engine. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live ⌘ T, rename the track REF (⌘ R), drag your chosen Black Propaganda track in, and loop three to four characteristic minutes.
  2. Live Audio Effects → Utility, trim to peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.
  3. Live REF stays one solo-press away all lesson.
  4. Digitakt II Press [RECORD] for grid recording and build a two-bar loop: kick on the quarters, a closed hat figure, one mid perc, and one short grey stab — a single repeated pitch, no interval, no chord.
  5. Digitakt II Deliberately monochrome; if any element is interesting alone, dull it.
  6. Live Check coverage against REF: your loop needs energy in the lows, mids, and highs, because the tilt is about to trade them against each other.
  7. Live A loop missing its top half gives the tilt nothing to brighten.
  8. Live Set levels by the ledger, the loop peaking ≈ −12 dBFS into Live.
  9. Live Run it static for two minutes; it should hold together and bore you slightly.
  10. Digitakt II + Logbook Save the pattern and declare it frozen in the logbook, with the timestamp.
  11. Digitakt II From this line on, the Digitakt II’s trig keys are out of bounds.
Expected result

The record loops at ≈ −12 dBFS, a grey two-bar engine runs beside it with all three spectral regions populated, and a written freeze line makes the pattern untouchable.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The loop is interesting on its ownA melodic interval or a colorful sample crept inFlatten it — one pitch for the stab, plainer samples; color belongs to the treatments here
The loop falls apart after a minute of listeningToo sparse — treatments cannot rescue a loop with holes in its grooveTighten the figure before freezing; the engine must run on its own, just without color
Phase 2 · ≈ 20 min

Wire the three lights

Phase focus: wire the three lights. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Digitakt II + Live On the Digitakt II’s channel in Live, drop an EQ Eight: band 1 a low shelf at ≈ 200 Hz, band 8 a high shelf at ≈ 3 kHz.
  2. Live Group the device (⌘ G) and map both shelf gains to one macro, inverted against each other — low shelf +3 → −3 dB as the high shelf runs −3 → +3 dB.
  3. Live One knob now tilts the whole loop dark-to-lit.
  4. Digitakt II + Live Create a return with a dark room reverb — decay around 2 s, damped highs — and send the whole Digitakt II channel to it.
  5. Live The send amount is control two.
  6. MicroFreak Make a noise track: a minute of MicroFreak noise-oscillator output printed once (or a noise sample), looped, low-passed around 4 kHz so it reads as air rather than hiss.
  7. MicroFreak Its fader is control three; it starts at silence.
  8. Live Sweep each control alone through its full range while the loop runs, and set its endpoints: the most extreme tilt, send, and noise level you would stand behind.
  9. Logbook Write the three endpoint values down — the ride stays inside them.
  10. Live Map all three to physical controls (⌘ M) — three knobs or faders under two hands.
Expected result

Three mapped global controls with written endpoints — a tilt that repaints every element at once, a room the whole loop shares, and a noise floor waiting at silence.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The tilt reads as a filter sweep on one elementShelf frequencies sit on top of a single part — usually the hatMove the shelves outward (lower low, higher high) until the move repaints everything equally
The tilt changes loudness, not colorShelf gains not inverted — both move the same directionRe-map the macro so one shelf falls as the other rises; the level should stay roughly constant
The noise reads as a mistake, not weatherUnfiltered hiss in the same register as the hatsLow-pass it further and keep its ceiling low — it is a floor under the scene, never a feature
Phase 3 · ≈ 30 min

The four-minute ride

Phase focus: the four-minute ride. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Plan the light arc on paper as three lines against minutes — for example: tilt dark → flat by 1:30 → lit by 3:00 → falling dark to the end;
  2. room low → swelling mid-piece → drying out; noise silent → audible only in the lit middle → gone.
  3. One move at a time where possible; this is still Block 1.
  4. Live Set the loop running, arm four minutes of Arrangement recording, and ride the three controls in one take, automation recorded.
  5. Digitakt II No mutes, no trig keys, no fourth control.
  6. Digitakt II Mistakes stay.
  7. Digitakt II Every move spread over at least 30 seconds — the record’s light changes at weather speed, and a fast tilt reads as a DJ move, which is a different record.
  8. Digitakt II Stay inside the written endpoints from phase 2. The temptation at minute three is to push the tilt past its endpoint for a climax;
  9. Digitakt II the record never does — its restraint is the grey.
Expected result

A four-minute take whose entire event list is three slow global lines — a piece that audibly develops while the Digitakt II provably repeats the same two bars throughout.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The piece still feels staticMoves too timid — endpoints set inside the audible rangeWiden the endpoints in phase 2 and re-ride; the panels of the diagram should be plainly different scenes
A move pops out as an eventA ride completed in seconds instead of half-minutesRedo that passage slower — the hand should move at weather speed, not gesture speed
A pattern edit happened mid-takeThe frozen loop felt like it needed “one more hat”Revert to the saved pattern and go again — the urge to edit is the lesson arriving; ride through it
Phase 4 · ≈ 15 min

Prove the pattern never changed

Phase focus: prove the pattern never changed. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Bounce 30 seconds from the start of the take and 30 seconds from the end.
  2. Live Put them on two tracks with a Utility each, trimmed until they read equally loud.
  3. Live Diff by ear, flipping between them: every kick, hat, perc, and stab should land in exactly the same place.
  4. Live Same events — different light.
  5. Live Then look: the waveforms agree on timing and disagree on shape.
  6. Live A/B against REF at matched loudness: is your light as slow, is your loop as grey, does your motion stay global?
  7. Logbook Write the logbook line: name what carried the form.
  8. Logbook The honest answer is the treatments — three knobs, no notes — and writing it fixes the lesson.
Expected result

Two loudness-matched bounces with identical event lists and different spectra, an A/B that comes down to slowness and greyness, and a logbook line crediting the form to the treatments.

If it fails
SymptomCauseFix
The two bounces differ in events, not just lightA pattern edit or mute slipped into the takeThe take is disqualified — revert to the frozen pattern and re-ride; the proof must come out clean
The two bounces sound identicalThe ride never accumulated — the controls returned to start before the endRe-plan the arc so the end state differs from the start; a round trip to zero erases the evidence
Verify against the record

Solo-flip between REF and your take at matched loudness — phones at the mark, no touching while you judge.

  • Did the pattern ever change?
    Answer

    No — and you proved it rather than claimed it: the two loudness-matched bounces carry the same event list in different spectra. If the diff turned up a changed event, a hand reached the pattern mid-take and the proof failed with the discipline. Relisten to the record running the same diff in your head across its first and last minute; its engine passes the same test.

  • What carried the form?
    Answer

    The treatments — a tilt, a room, a noise floor, ridden slowly inside written endpoints. Name them in the logbook as what they are: the entire arrangement, performed after the engine. If your answer wants to credit the loop, flip once more between your two bounces — everything that differs between them is treatment, and everything that differs is the form.

  • Logbook — three-line miss list. Matched: where your version produces the record’s effect. Missed: where it doesn’t — and your best guess why. Change next: the one move you’d try first.
  • These three lines feed the full miss list in replication lab R1.
  • Carries into the lab: arrangement can live entirely in the master’s color while the engine just runs — in Lab 003 terms, an engine that operates after the channel, in the global chain, and the loop test gets a corollary: a loop that survives bare will survive any light you put it under.
