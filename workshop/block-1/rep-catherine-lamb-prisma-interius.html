Replicate: tuning as the only variable

Copy one clear section of Catherine Lamb — Prisma Interius V. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

OverviewListeningLabsRebuildCapstoneRep lessonsDashboard
The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: sustained two-note dyads tuned to just-intonation ratios, where whether you hear pure consonance or gentle beating is decided purely by the tuning ratio — not by effects, not by performance. The ratio is the entire instrument. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

Before you start
  • Gear: MicroFreak → M4 input 1, DT 990 Pro in the phones jack — the lab-001 wiring, unchanged; the Digitakt II rests this lesson
  • New set saved as REP-LAMB — no tempo worth setting; the variable is the cent value of each interval
  • Pure sustained dyads are deceptive — a locked just interval can be louder than it meters because nothing is fighting it. Phones at the lab-001 mark or lower, a real break every 15 minutes
  • Timebox: 60–90 minutes, then file it and stop — the time goes into tuning two notes precisely, not adding a third
  • What you will make: a short replica of Catherine Lamb — Prisma Interius V. Focus on this effect: held dyads that lock into stillness or breathe with shimmer, decided only by tuning.
  • What you will turn off: chorus, detune effects, vibrato, anything that adds movement the tuning didn’t put there.
  • What you will prove: your version and REF use the same core mechanism: just-intonation dyads where consonance and beating are set purely by tuning ratios.
  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Catherine Lamb — Prisma Interius V. Choose the clearest sustained dyad. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. How many notes are sounding at once?
    Answer

    Usually two — a dyad, held. Not a thick cluster like Niblock, but a single clean interval. The whole drama is in that one interval and how it’s tuned.

  2. Does the dyad sit perfectly still, or does it shimmer?
    Answer

    Both happen, in different places. When an interval is tuned to a simple just ratio it locks into a glassy stillness with almost no beating; when it’s tuned to a more complex ratio it breathes with a gentle, slow shimmer. The stillness and the shimmer are two settings of the same control.

  3. What decides whether it’s still or shimmering?
    Answer

    The tuning ratio, and nothing else. A 3:2 fifth or a 5:4 third locks; a 7:6 or 11:8 breathes. There is no effect adding the movement — the interval’s own arithmetic does it. Tune it differently and the character changes completely.

  4. Is the shimmer an effect like chorus?
    Answer

    No. It is acoustic beating between the two tones, the same physics as Niblock’s throb but with only two notes, so it reads as a delicate breathing rather than a dense pulse. No chorus, no detune plugin — just the ratio.

  5. Where is the composition?
    Answer

    In the sequence of ratios. Moving from one dyad to another tuned dyad — from a locked fifth to a breathing seventh, say — is the entire compositional move. The score is a list of intervals expressed as ratios, and every quality you hear falls out of them.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Live 12 tuning: set precise just intervals with Utility → Pitch (cents) on the upper voice, or use a tuning/scale device. A just fifth is +2 cents over equal-tempered; a just major third is −14 cents; a 7/4 is −31 cents. The cents are the score.
  • MicroFreak: hold one note as the root; print the second note as a separate take, using the fine-tune to land the exact interval in cents. Keep the engine clean, no chorus, no LFO — the tuning is the only variable.
  • Digitakt II: idle this lesson — there is no rhythm; the music is in the held intervals.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
  • Sound target: a solo-flip between REF and your piece lands on the same purity — held dyads that either lock into stillness or breathe with a slow shimmer, decided only by tuning.
  • Two notes at a time and nothing else — no chorus, no vibrato, no third voice to thicken it.
  • The consonance and the beating come entirely from the ratio; the same notes at a different ratio are a different piece.
  • Every change is a cent value, not an effect — if you reach for a plugin to add shimmer, the lesson failed.

Phase 1 · ≈ 10 min

The reference and the track

Phase focus: the reference and the track. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. ⌘ T, rename the track REF (⌘ R), and drag a section of Prisma Interius V into it.
  2. Loop a two-to-three-minute stretch where you can hear at least one locked dyad and one that breathes.
  3. Drop Audio Effects → Utility on REF and trim so it peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.
  4. REF stays one solo-press away for the whole lesson.
  5. ⌘ T for the root, Ext. In 1, fader at 0 dB, armed; name it ROOT.
  6. Write in the logbook, for each clear dyad, whether it locks or breathes, and — if it breathes — roughly how slow the shimmer is.
  7. Keep those notes; phase 3 will choose ratios to reproduce that lock-and-breathe sequence.
  1. Work in Live 12 only for this phase: keep REF looped from Catherine Lamb — Prisma Interius V and use two clean sustained tones with the upper voice tuned in cents.
  2. Build the phase goal — The reference and the track — as the smallest Live set that proves consonance and beating are set purely by tuning ratios.
  3. Keep every source on named tracks, then use Utility to match the level against REF before judging it.
  4. Record or resample the result to a fresh audio track named PRINT; do not leave the proof only as devices and MIDI.
  5. A/B ten seconds of PRINT against REF and change only the cents that most affect lock vs breathe.
  1. Use Digitone II for this phase: two clean FM voices, the upper tuned precisely in cents to a just ratio; record main-out.
  2. Program it so it demonstrates tuning ratios deciding consonance and beating, not just the surface of Prisma Interius V.
  3. Keep one clear performance control — the upper voice’s interval in cents — and name it before recording.
  4. Record the device into Live or a recorder as a full pass, then compare with REF at matched level.
  5. Use a target-box shortcut only when the same mechanism stays audible.
Expected result

A reference loop at ≈ −12 dBFS with at least one locked dyad and one that breathes, one named root track waiting, and a lock-vs-breathe note for each dyad on paper.

Phase 2 · ≈ 15 min

The two tones — root and a tuned partner

Phase focus: the two tones. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. MicroFreak: pick a clean engine with a clear fundamental and a few harmonics, and hold the root note — full sustain, no chorus, no LFO.
  2. Print 1–2 minutes of the root into ROOT, peaking ≈ −12 dBFS.
  3. ⌘ T, name it PARTNER, Ext. In 1. Print a second held tone a just fifth above — set the fine-tune so the interval is +2 cents over the equal-tempered fifth, the pure 3:2.
  4. Solo the two together and listen: a true 3:2 should lock into glassy stillness with almost no beating.
  5. Now print a second version of PARTNER tuned to a 7:6 or 7:4 (about −31 cents from the nearest equal-tempered note) and hear it breathe.
  6. Drop each fader so the dyad peaks ≈ −12 dBFS together. Confirm: same two engines, only the cents differ between the locked and breathing versions.
  1. Work in Live 12 only for this phase: a held root and a second voice tuned with Utility Pitch (cents) to exact just ratios.
  2. Build the phase goal — the tuned dyad — as the smallest Live set that proves the quality is in the ratio.
  3. Keep both on named tracks, then use Utility to match the dyad level against REF before judging it.
  4. Resample to a fresh audio track named PRINT; do not leave the proof only as devices and MIDI.
  5. A/B a locked vs a breathing ratio against REF and confirm the only difference is cents.
  1. Use Digitone II for this phase: two FM voices, the upper tuned precisely to the just ratio in cents.
  2. Program it so it demonstrates a dyad whose character is set by tuning, not just the surface of Prisma Interius V.
  3. Keep the interval-in-cents as the one named control for later.
  4. Record the dyad into Live or a recorder, then compare with REF at matched level.
  5. Use a shortcut only when the same lock/breathe distinction stays audible.
Expected result

Two clean tones forming a dyad, with at least two tuned versions of the partner — one that locks (a simple ratio) and one that breathes (a complex ratio) — proving the quality is in the cents, not in any effect. The dyad peaks ≈ −12 dBFS.

Phase 3 · ≈ 25 min

Compose with ratios only

Phase focus: compose with ratios only. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Write a ratio sequence on paper: three or four dyads, each named by its ratio — “3:2 (lock) → 5:4 (lock, sweeter) → 7:6 (breathe) → 11:8 (breathe faster).” That list is the score.
  2. Build each dyad by holding the root and printing the partner at the cents for that ratio; lay them as long held sections in Arrangement.
  3. To move between dyads, let the old partner fade and the new tuned partner enter — or, for a Lamb-like seamlessness, slowly retune the partner from one ratio to the next over many seconds so the lock dissolves into a breathe and back.
  4. Add no effects, no third voice, no rhythm. Every compositional decision is a ratio.
  5. Keep the level steady — this is not a level piece; the content is the tuning.
  6. The rule: if you want more movement, you choose a more complex ratio. You never reach for a modulator.
  1. Work in Live 12 only for this phase: set each section’s partner to the ratio’s cents; optionally automate the cents slowly to glide between ratios.
  2. Build the phase goal — compose with ratios only — as the smallest Live set that proves the ratio sequence is the composition.
  3. Keep both voices on named tracks, then use Utility to match the level against REF before judging it.
  4. Resample to PRINT; do not leave the proof only as devices and MIDI.
  5. A/B against REF and adjust only the cents.
  1. Use Digitone II for this phase: program each section’s interval in cents; automate the upper voice’s tuning to glide between ratios.
  2. Program it so it demonstrates ratios as the composition, not just the surface of Prisma Interius V.
  3. Keep the interval-in-cents as the named, repeatable control.
  4. Record a full pass into Live or a recorder, then compare with REF at matched level.
  5. Use a shortcut only when the same ratio-driven character stays audible.
Expected result

A 2–4 minute piece whose entire content is a sequence of held just-intonation dyads — some locking into stillness, some breathing — with every change expressible as a ratio and no effect anywhere.

Phase 4 · ≈ 15 min

Hold it, A/B it, and trust the arithmetic

Phase focus: hold it, A/B it, and trust the arithmetic. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Play your piece end to end, no touching, at the mark or lower.
  2. If the urge to add chorus to make the locked dyads “richer” arrives, it is the lesson arriving — the lock is the richness.
  3. A/B one minute of yours against REF at matched loudness, judging two things: do your locks sit as still, and do your breathing dyads shimmer as gently rather than clash.
  4. For each dyad, confirm the quality matches your phase-1 reading: was the locked one locked, the breathing one breathing?
  5. Write in the logbook how a couple of cents changed the entire character of a dyad — the smallest control with the largest effect.
  6. Take the scheduled break. Pure dyads can feel quiet yet load the ear over a long hold.
  1. Work in Live 12 only for this phase: judge the print against REF at matched level.
  2. Confirm the smallest Live set still proves tuning ratios decide consonance and beating.
  3. Use Utility to match the level against REF before judging.
  4. Keep the proof as a printed PRINT track.
  5. Adjust only the cents that set each dyad’s character.
  1. Use Digitone II for this phase: verify each dyad’s interval and any glide hold across the pass.
  2. Confirm the program demonstrates ratios as the only variable.
  3. Keep the interval-in-cents as the named, repeatable control.
  4. Record the full pass and compare with REF at matched level.
  5. Use a shortcut only when the same mechanism stays audible.
Expected result

A piece whose every quality traces to a tuning ratio, an A/B that comes down to how still the locks are and how gently the breathing dyads shimmer, and a logbook line about how a few cents rewrote a dyad’s entire character.

If it fails

The breathing dyads sound out of tune rather than aliveThe complex ratio is too far off, reading as a wrong noteChoose a recognised just ratio (7:6, 7:4, 11:8) so the beating is musical, not error

Verify against the record

Solo-flip between REF and your piece at matched loudness — phones at the mark or lower, no level riding while you judge.

  • Could a listener find an effect adding the movement?
    Answer

    They should not be able to, in your piece or the record. If a breathing dyad’s shimmer turns out to be chorus, the tuning isn’t doing the work and that section goes back to phase 2 — every shimmer must be acoustic beating from the ratio.

  • Did the tuning alone produce both the stillness and the shimmer?
    Answer

    That is the test. With effects banned and only two notes at a time, the ratio decides everything — a simple ratio locks, a complex one breathes. If your locks were glassy and your breathing dyads alive, the arithmetic carried the piece. If a dyad fell flat, the fix is never an effect; it is a better-chosen ratio. Relisten to the record: every quality you hear is two tones tuned to a number.
