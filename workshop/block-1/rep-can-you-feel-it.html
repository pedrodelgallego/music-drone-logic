Block 1 · Replication lesson

Replicate: swing you can set

Copy one clear section of Mr. Fingers — “Can You Feel It”. Build the main parts, record a short take, and compare it to the reference at the same loudness.

The point

This drill teaches the record's core mechanism: a machine swing setting - one rule applied to every offbeat. Do only the steps that make that mechanism audible.

  • What counts as success: remaining differences are source choices or rig limits, not missing steps.
Reference pass · 10 minutes

Listen before you build

Listen to about one minute of Mr. Fingers — “Can You Feel It”. Choose the clearest short section. Write one short answer for each question before you touch the gear.

  1. Which hits lean — and which hold the frame?
    Answer

    The offbeat hats lean; the kick and the bass hold the frame. Every hit on a downbeat sits on the grid, and every hit between downbeats arrives a chosen amount late - that split is the whole definition of swing.

  2. Roughly how much do they lean — a subtle lilt, or a hard shuffle?
    Answer

    A lilt - well short of a shuffle. The offbeats are unmistakably late once you listen for them, but a casual ear just hears “warm” rather than “swung”.

  3. Is the lean identical every bar?
    Answer

    Yes - machine swing is. A drum machine applies the same delay to every offbeat by rule, so the lean cannot vary unless someone changes the setting. That perfect sameness is what separates dialed swing from a drummer’s swing, which breathes.

  4. Does the bassline swing with the hats, or sit straight?
    Answer

    Listen to where its notes start: the bass moves mostly on and around the downbeats, so it reads as part of the straight frame, while the hats do the leaning above it. That contrast matters - if everything swung, nothing would.

Now build the section you described. Do not build from memory.

Steps

  • Button map for the steps below: use these controls whenever the lesson names the device.
  • Live 12: ⌘ T creates an audio track, ⇧⌘ T creates a MIDI track, ⌘ R renames the selected track, ⌘ , opens Settings, ⌘ M enters MIDI Map mode, and ⌘ J consolidates the selected clip.
  • Digitakt II: [TRK] + [TRIG 1-16] selects the active track. Press [RECORD] for grid recording, press the numbered [TRIG] keys to place or remove hits, and press [PLAY] to hear the pattern.
  • Digitakt II timing: press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to set swing. 50% is no swing. In grid recording, hold a trig and press [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to check or zero micro-timing.
  • Digitakt II sound and sync: press [SRC] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to choose a sample. Press [FX] to reach delay/reverb/chorus sends. Press [SETTINGS] for MIDI Config → Sync; use [YES] or [LEFT]/[RIGHT] to toggle receive settings.
  • MicroFreak: select the oscillator engine with the Type control, then set cutoff, resonance, envelope, LFO, and volume with the front-panel knobs before recording. When the page gives a value, move that knob slowly while A/Bing against REF.
  • Recorder or phone: press record once to arm or meter if your device has a standby state, set gain before the take, press record again to capture, and do not touch the gain during the take unless the lesson explicitly tells you to restart.
  • Sound target: the record’s pattern and bassline, with every offbeat leaning by one rule — and the rule set to the record’s amount.
  • The lean is identical every bar, because a setting applies it — you chose the number by ear, against the record.
  • You can name the number, and you heard what one step above and below it feels like.
Phase 1 · ~30 min

Transcribe and program the section — dead straight

Phase focus: transcribe and program the section — dead straight. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Live Drop “Can You Feel It” into Live on a track named REF and loop the 8–16 bars you chose in the reference pass.
  2. Live Gain-match it with a Utility and keep it one solo away all sitting.
  3. Live Set Live’s tempo to the record’s by ear — tap Tap along with REF until the metronome agrees.
  4. Digitakt II Transcribe their drum pattern by ear on the Digitakt II — kick, hats, clap — picking the closest sounds to their kit.
  5. Digitakt II Press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D until SWING reads 50%.
  6. Digitakt II Confirm SWING still reads 50% before you leave the tempo page. On the Digitakt II, 50% means no swing.
  7. MicroFreak Transcribe the bassline on the MicroFreak — a few notes, looping;
  8. Live sequence it from a Live MIDI clip so it locks to the grid.
  9. Live Match their patch roughly: soft attack, round and warm.
  10. Digitakt II + Live Loop yours against REF and correct voice by voice until the pattern is the record’s.
  11. Digitakt II + Live Keep everything straight — the lean comes in Phase 2, as one setting.

One plausible transcription shape — yours may differ; the grid is the method, not the answer. The hats are marked dim on purpose: they sit on those offbeat cells now, but at the record’s setting they live a rule-sized distance after them. The grid cannot draw that — the swing setting will.

Expected result

A dry, straight version of the record’s section in your sounds. Against REF it should already be recognizably the same music — and noticeably stiffer, because their offbeats lean and yours do not yet. That stiffness is the number you are about to find.

Phase 2 · ~25 min

Step the swing amount and feel each number

Phase focus: step the swing amount and feel each number. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Digitakt II Pick one swing method and stick to it.
  2. Digitakt II Digitakt II: per-track swing on the hat track (and any other offbeat voice).
  3. Live Live: a groove from the Groove Pool applied to your clips, Timing at full.
  4. Live One method, not both.
  5. Live Mute REF.
  6. Digitakt II Press [TEMPO] and set SWING to 50%. Play a few bars. Then set SWING to 54%, 58%, and 62% in separate listening passes.
  7. Logbook At each stop, listen for at least eight bars and write one word for the feel — your word, not the manual’s.
  8. Logbook Do not skip a stop and do not rush one; the differences are small until they are suddenly not.
  9. Logbook Note the setting where the loop stops feeling stiff — and the setting where it starts feeling drunk.
  10. Live The record lives between those two.

Expected result

Four distinct feels from one pattern and one knob: flat, warming, rolling, staggering — in your own words. The realization to write down: nothing about the pattern changed. The feel is the number.

If it fails

Phase 3 · ~20 min

Find the record’s setting by ear

Phase focus: find the record’s setting by ear. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Do this
  1. Digitakt II + Live Unmute REF and loop it against your pattern.
  2. Digitakt II + Live Start from the Phase 2 setting that felt closest.
  3. Live A/B a few bars at a time: are your offbeats leaning less than theirs, the same, or more?
  4. Digitakt II Press [TEMPO], turn DATA ENTRY knob D by one or two percent, and listen again.
  5. Digitakt II When you think you have it, overshoot by one step on purpose, hear the difference, and come back.
  6. Digitakt II A match you cannot overshoot was found by luck.
  7. Logbook Write the final number in the logbook before Phase 4 touches anything.
Expected result

One setting where your loop and the record lean together — the stiffness gone, the stagger never arrived. You can name the number, and you know what one step either side of it sounds like.

Phase 4 · ~20 min

Record it and measure the rule in ms

Phase focus: record it and measure the rule in ms. Follow the steps in order, then compare against REF before moving on.

Digitakt II · swing ___% → main outs → M4 in 3/4 → Live record · Ext. In 3/4 · Monitor Off
Do this
  1. Digitakt II + Live Restart the transport — the Lab 002 reset rule — then record 8 bars at swing 50% into Live (Ext. In 3/4, Monitor Off).
  2. Digitakt II + Live Press [TEMPO] and turn DATA ENTRY knob D to the matched swing value. Record the same 8 bars again onto a second Live track after another transport restart.
  3. Live Observe — see it, not just hear it. Set the ruler to minutes:seconds:milliseconds, as in Lab 002, and zoom both takes to the same offbeat hat.
  4. Live Measure each hat’s distance from the grid line and subtract: the 50% hat sits on the line, the swung hat sits ___ ms after it.
  5. Live Check a second offbeat in another bar — the difference must be identical, because a rule put it there.
Expected result

One number in milliseconds, the same at every offbeat you check. Swing demystified: the warmth you matched by ear is that number, applied by rule — found with your ears, stated as engineering.

Verify against the record

A/B your matched recording against the REF section, then answer:

  • Is your lean identical every bar — and is it the same lean as theirs? Loop 4 bars of each and listen only to the hats.
    Answer

    Identical every bar should be automatic — a setting applied it, and machine swing cannot wander. So if the two loops still differ, the miss is the amount, not the consistency: your number is a step or two from theirs. Go back to single-percent steps around your setting rather than doubting the method, and relisten to hats only until the two leans land together.

  • 10 seconds of yours, 10 of theirs — would a friend hesitate to say which is which? Do both move your hips the same way?
    Answer

    At the right setting the body answers before the ear does — the same lilt shows up in your shoulders for both loops. If the friend would not hesitate, the giveaway is usually timbre or the bassline’s phrasing rather than the swing number, because the number is the easiest part to match exactly. Name the real difference out loud and write it in the miss list, then relisten asking only: do the offbeats of both loops land together?

  • Logbook — the miss list, three lines: Matched: ___ · Missed: ___ (best guess why) · Change next: ___
  • These three lines feed the full miss list in replication lab R1.
  • What carries into the lab: swing is a parameter with a value, not a vibe — and from now on you set it on purpose, like a buffer size.
