Block 5 — Space & Mix

Effects are routing architectures, not inserts

Delay, reverb, feedback, width, and subtraction — the block where you stop adding effects to tracks and start designing where sound travels. Every space a record puts you in is a path somebody wired; this block teaches you to draw that path, ride it, and prove it survives the trip to a phone speaker.

Year 2 · Domain D · Labs 018–022 · Replication R5 · Programme L5 · closes at gate capstone C4

Engineering focus

An effect is a routing architecture, not a device on a track. Required concepts: insert vs send · pre- vs post-fader · return level vs feedback amount · the wet/dry ambiguity (is the space in the sound, or beside it?) · phase and correlation · mono collapse · room vs headphones · translation across playback systems. Every lab in this block asks the same question first: where does the signal go, and where does it come back in?

Working order

Do these in this order
  1. Programme L5 — run the three listening sessions first and name one stolen principle
  2. Lab 018 — compose with a delay send; the send fader is the writing surface
  3. Lab 019 — play a reverb as an instrument and print its space as material
  4. Lab 020 — wire feedback paths; the safety checklist runs before any sound
  5. Lab 021 — bench: mono, width, phase, room — the translation deep dive
  6. Lab 022 — arrange by removal; mixing as subtraction
  7. R5 — rebuild one dub passage and verify the L5 analysis
  8. Capstone C4 — the gate: a dub-system piece, translation-tested
  • Safety first at lab 020. Feedback paths are wired as both a musical system and a safety-critical system. The in-lab checklist — limiter location, emergency mute, send amount, return level, monitoring level, pre/post-fader status, maximum safe test procedure — runs before sound is made, every session, no exceptions. The same checklist applies whenever a feedback path is armed in R5 or C4.
  • Year 2 buffer rule. After C4 (or after lab 024, whichever lands when fatigue shows), insert one non-portfolio cycle: repair, remix, documentation cleanup, or a Studio Signal Map revision. The buffer is part of the plan, not a failure of it.

Listening programme L5 — space as instrument

Session 1 · Records

Four spaces, four architectures

Four records where the space is the composition — from the Kingston desk that invented the method to the Detroit-Berlin lineage that abstracted it. Analyze each with the six-question frame, then compare the architectures.

Augustus Pablo / King Tubby — “King Tubby Meets Rockers Uptown”the desk as instrument
Rhythm & Sound w/ Cornell Campbell — “King in My Empire”dub as stillness
Maurizio — “M5”the chord and the send
Deepchord presents Echospace — The Coldest Seasonspace as the whole foreground
Listen for

What is dry, what is sent, and who decides when? In each record, find the moment a sound is thrown into the space — and ask whether the space is an effect on the music or the music itself.

Session 2 · Samples

Deconstruction — the send/return choreography

Pick one dub-system piece and map its send/return choreography: which elements are dry, which are sent, to what, when, and how much — throw by throw. The worksheet becomes the blueprint for replication lab R5.

Session 3 · Video

Dub-desk footage — Scientist at the console

Watched for send/return choreography, not nostalgia: where the hands are, which sends move and when, what gets muted, and what could never be recovered once the dub passed the tape head.

  • Deliverable: written system analyses (six questions per reference) + one stolen principle — a structural idea, never material, named in advance and evaluated at C4. Full briefs and worksheets: programme L5 scaffold

Capstone C4 — gate

Capstone C4 · Gate

A dub-system piece, translation-tested

Constraints only, no recipe. The System Spec must draw the full send/return/feedback architecture before anything is patched; the finished mix is verified across monitors, mono, and a phone speaker, with a written translation report. The L5 stolen principle comes due, the fault is phase, feedback routing, or translation, and the seven defense prompts close the block. Full brief: capstone C4 scaffold
