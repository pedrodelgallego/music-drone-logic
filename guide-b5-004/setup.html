Translation & Subtraction · 04 — Setup

The rig that lets you hear the fold

Translation is a measurement discipline, so the setup is about instrumentation, not new instruments. You need three things wired and ready before the build: a master metering chain that shows mono, correlation, and spectrum at a glance; a one-button mono switch; and a reference playback chain that gets the render onto a phone and into a room with no friction.

The signal path you are auditing

Everything funnels to one stereo master, and the master is where translation is judged. Build the metering on the master bus once, leave it there, and read it constantly.

All tracks → Group buses → Master → Utility (mono switch) → Spectrum + correlation → M4 Phones / monitors

Master metering chain — build it once

Setup 01

Utility + Spectrum on the master

Drop the devices, in this order
  • Select the Master track. Add a Utility first, then a Spectrum after it. Nothing else — no limiter, no EQ on the master for this exercise.
  • The Utility is your monitoring tool, not a processor: leave Gain at 0 dB and Width at 100 % by default. You will only ever touch its Mono switch.
  • Spectrum after Utility so the analyser shows exactly what leaves the master — including whatever the mono switch does to it.
Map the mono switch to one button
  • ⌘ M for MIDI map mode. Click the Utility's Mono button, then tap one pad on the Launchpad Pro MK3. Exit map mode.
  • No controller? Right-click the Mono button → Edit MIDI Map, or bind it to a key with the Key map (⌘ K) — any single key you can hit without looking.
  • The goal is a reflex: one button, mono on, listen, button off. If checking mono takes more than a finger, you will stop doing it.
Read the correlation
  • In Spectrum, enable the stereo/correlation display if your version shows it; otherwise add a dedicated correlation meter (any free phase meter, or Ableton's metering view) after Spectrum.
  • Position the meter where you can see it while your hands are on the faders — a translation problem you cannot see is a translation problem you will ship.
  • Healthy target: the needle living around +0.3 to +0.8, dipping briefly on reverb tails, never parking below 0 on a load-bearing part.
Decision check
  • Can you toggle mono with one button without taking your eyes off the meters?
  • Is the master chain monitoring-only — no limiter, no master EQ baked in?
  • Can you see correlation and spectrum at the same time as the mix?

Per-channel translation tools

Setup 02

Mono the lows, M/S where it counts

Utility Bass Mono on the low-end bus
  • On the group that carries kick and bass, add a Utility and enable Bass Mono (the "mono frequency" control). Set the crossover around 120 Hz.
  • Everything below the crossover is forced to centre; everything above keeps its stereo image. The bass survives the fold while the mids stay wide.
  • This is a set-and-forget insurance device. You are not mixing with it — you are removing the possibility of a low-end phase problem.
EQ Eight in M/S mode for surgical width
Where M/S EQ earns its place
Side, low shelf cutPull side energy below ~200 Hz to zero even if Bass Mono is already doing the job — belt and braces on the most fragile band.
Side, high shelf boostAdd air and width up top where phase problems are inaudible — the safe place to widen.
Mid, narrow cutCarve a masking band out of the centred core without touching the width.
  • In EQ Eight, switch the editing mode chooser from Stereo (L/R) to M/S, then pick Mid or Side per band. Reversible, surgical, and exactly the tool the decisions page argued for.

The reference playback chain

Setup 03

Make cross-system listening frictionless

The three judges, ready to hand
Reference playback chain
HeadphonesYour open-back studio set (DT 990 Pro) on the M4 phones out — the detail and phase judge.
Phone speakerAn AirDrop / cable transfer folder so the render lands on your phone in seconds. The single mono speaker is your harshest judge.
RoomMonitors, or a small Bluetooth speaker in another room — the depth and balance judge, distrusted for absolute bass.
  • Have a known commercial reference loaded on all three too. You are checking your track against records that already translate — not against silence.
Render settings that protect the test
Export for translation listening
File Type / Sample Rate / Bit DepthWAV, 48000 Hz, 24-bit
NormalizeOFF — the headroom you protected by cutting is part of the result
Convert to MonoOFF — fold to mono with the Utility switch, not the renderer, so you keep one stereo file
Limiter on masterNone — loudness is a distribution problem; this exercise is about translation, not level
Decision check
  • Can a fresh render reach your phone and a second room in under a minute? If not, you will skip the test under deadline.
  • Is there a commercial reference cued on each system to A/B against?
  • Is the master clean — no normalize, no limiter — so what you hear is the mix, not a processor hiding its faults?
  • Set the listening level once. Translation judgements drift with volume. Pick a comfortable monitoring level on the M4 phones knob and leave it; ride the mix faders, never the listening level.
  • One clock, one master, one mono button. The whole rig exists so that the only variable you change between systems is the system — never the file, never the gain.
