Lab Note B3-004
Euclidean · 04 — Setup

The rig that generates the rule

There are two honest ways to make Euclidean patterns in this studio: place the computed string by hand on the Digitakt II and exploit per-track length, or let an Ableton Euclidean MIDI generator compute it and drive the MicroFreak. This page wires both, and the clock that keeps them locked.

The two engines

Digitakt II — length is the trick

  • No dedicated Euclidean mode
  • You place the computed string by hand, once
  • Per-track length makes each track its own M — Euclidean and polymeter at once

Ableton — the generator computes it

  • Live 12 MIDI Generators include a Euclidean Generator
  • Set steps, pulses/fills, and rotation as live values
  • The MIDI clip drives the MicroFreak so the bass inherits the rule

Where the Euclidean tools live

Generating Euclidean patterns with what you have
Ableton — GenerateLive 12's MIDI Generators include a Euclidean Generator. In a MIDI clip, open the Generate panel and choose Euclidean: set Steps (M), Fills/Pulses (N), and Rotation. It writes the notes into the clip.
Ableton — Max for LiveSeveral free M4L Euclidean sequencers give you hits, steps, and rotation as live knobs you can map and automate — useful when you want the integer itself to move over the arrangement.
Digitakt IINo Euclidean mode. You read the computed string off page 05 and place trigs by hand, then exploit per-track length (TRK LEN / pattern scale page) so each track is its own E(N,M) of a different M.
MicroFreakDrive its sound from an Ableton Euclidean MIDI clip over USB, so the bass inherits the same rule as the drums. Its onboard arp/sequencer can also be triggered, but the cleanest path is the Ableton clip.

Signal & control path

Control — where the rule is computed and sent

Ableton Euclidean Generator → MIDI clip → MIDI track → USB → MicroFreak (bass voice) + clock → Digitakt II (hand-placed strings)

Audio — where the sound is recorded

MicroFreak Out → TS → M4 In 1 + Digitakt II Main L/R → 2 × TRS → M4 In 3/4 → USB-C → Ableton → DT 990 Pro
One clock to rule them all

Ableton is the only clock master. The Digitakt II receives clock and transport; the MicroFreak receives clock and the Euclidean MIDI clip. If the Digitakt's tempo display disagrees with Ableton, stop and fix sync before recording — a pattern recorded against a drifting clock cannot be repaired.

Set up the Ableton Euclidean MIDI track

MicroFreak bass driven by the generator
1 — Create a MIDI track⌘ ⇧ T. Name it MF EUCLID. MIDI To → MicroFreak, channel 1.
2 — Make a 1-bar clipDouble-click an empty clip slot to create an empty MIDI clip, 1 bar long.
3 — GenerateOpen the clip, click Generate → Euclidean. Set Steps 16, Pulses 5, Rotation 0. One note pitch for a bass.
4 — Verify the stringThe clip should show hits matching E(5,16) on page 05. If it does not, your Pulses/Steps are swapped.
5 — Clock the MicroFreakOn the MicroFreak: Shift + Tempo → source USB. Press play in Ableton; the bass note should fire on the E(5,16) steps.

Set up the Digitakt II per-track lengths

Each track its own M — the polymeter superpower
T1 KICKLength 16. Hand-place E(4,16) — trigs on 1, 5, 9, 13. The anchor; do not vary it.
T2 HATLength 16. Hand-place E(7,16) from page 05 — the busy, even offbeat layer.
T3 PERCLength 11. Hand-place E(3,11). At 11 steps it phases against the 16-step kick and realigns every 176 steps — Euclidean and polymeter on one track.
T4 TEXTURELength 12. Hand-place E(5,12) — a West-African bell ancestor, drifting against the 16-grid.
  • Per-track length on Digitakt II: on the relevant track, open the pattern length / scale settings and switch from per-pattern to per-track scaling, then set that track's step count. This is what lets T3 run 11 steps while T1 runs 16.
  • Place the string, then forget it. Once a Euclidean line is entered by hand, treat the trigs as fixed. Evolution comes from changing one length or rotation later, not from nudging individual trigs.
Avoid the common setup traps
  • Generator wrote the wrong rhythm → Pulses (N) and Steps (M) are swapped, or Rotation is non-zero when you expected 0.
  • Digitakt track will not change its length → still in per-pattern scale mode; switch to per-track first.
  • MicroFreak ignores the clip → clock source not set to USB, or MIDI To is pointed at the wrong port/channel.
  • Polymeter never drifts → all tracks are still 16 steps; the drift only exists when M differs per track.
Before you move on
  • The Ableton Euclidean Generator writes the string I expect and the MicroFreak plays it.
  • Ableton is the sole clock master; the Digitakt II follows its tempo and transport.
  • At least one Digitakt track has a length other than 16, so polymeter is live.
  • Audio is recorded once through the M4; I am not monitoring two paths at once.
