Feedback Safety · 05 — Feedback states

The playable states, and how to find and hold each

A feedback instrument is not one sound — it is a small set of stable-enough states the loop can sit in, separated by transitions you ride between them. This page is the palette: each state, the safe range of the ride control where it lives, what it sounds like, and exactly how to find it and hold it. Learn these in rehearsal so that live, you are recalling positions rather than discovering them.

The states, low to high

The ride control from silent to capped — never beyond
S0 — SilentLoop gain below unity. Any sound dies away. The home base and the safe state.
S1 — BloomJust under unity. A tone fed in swells slowly, then settles. The gentlest usable state.
S2 — Held toneAt unity. A single pitch sustains steadily, neither growing nor dying. The plateau — the core of the instrument.
S3 — BrinkA hair above unity. The tone grows slowly and brightens; harmonics pile up. Tension lives here.
CAP — CeilingThe capped maximum, a clear margin below true runaway. The loudest the rig will physically allow. You visit, never live here.
The whole performance is between S1 and S3

S0 is where you start and where the panic path returns you. S2 is home. S1 and S3 are the two directions you can move from home. Anything past the cap does not exist on this instrument — by design.

State by state — find it, hold it, leave it

S0

Silent — home base

Find it
  • Ride control fully down, or panic pad engaged. Loop gain well below unity.
  • Tap a sound into the loop — it decays and is gone. That decay confirms you are below unity.
Hold it / leave it
  • It holds itself — nothing sustains. This is the only truly safe state.
  • Leave it by raising the ride control slowly toward S1, hand on the panic pad.
S1

Bloom — the swell that settles

Find it
  • Raise the ride control until a fed-in tone grows a little, then stops growing. The growth is the loop adding energy; the settling is it still being below true unity.
  • This is the largest part of the usable range — a gentle, forgiving region. On the encoder it is typically the lower-middle of the capped travel.
Hold it / leave it
  • Holds easily. Small filter moves here shift the pitch without risk. Good for long, slow passages.
  • Leave it upward into S2 by nudging the ride control a touch more; downward into S0 by pulling back.
S2

Held tone — the plateau

Find it
  • Edge the ride control up from S1 until a tone sustains without growing or fading. That steady hold is unity — the heart of the instrument.
  • The window is narrow. Learn the exact knob/fader position in rehearsal and mark it (tape on hardware, note the value on the mapped control).
  • The filter centre sets the pitch; confirm it lands on your set's key note.
Hold it / leave it
  • Holds, but watch it — drift in the room or gear can push it toward S3 on its own. A tone that slowly brightens means you have crept upward; ease the ride back a hair.
  • This is the state you return to between gestures. From here, S3 is up, S1 is down, and the panic pad is always one move away.
S3

Brink — the tension state

Find it
  • From S2, nudge the ride control up until the tone slowly grows and brightens — new harmonics climb in. This is just above unity.
  • It is rising toward the cap on its own. You do not park here — you pass through it on a swell and come back.
Hold it / leave it
  • Do not hold. S3 wants to climb. The musical move is to ride into it and back out — the brink-of-runaway tension is the event, living at the edge, never over it.
  • Leave it by pulling the ride control back to S2 or S1 before it reaches the cap. The limiter will catch you if you misjudge, but the music is in not needing it to.
  • If it gets away from you: panic pad. No hesitation. Recover to S0 and rebuild.

Shaping the tone within a state

Once you can find and hold the states, the filter and EQ give you colour without changing the danger level. These moves stay safe because they do not raise loop gain — they only move where the energy already in the loop sits.

Safe tone moves — they shift colour, not level
Filter centre frequencySlides the oscillation pitch. Sweep it slowly in S2 for a melodic glide between notes — the most musical single gesture on the instrument.
Filter resonance / QNarrow Q = purer, more sine-like (Sachiko territory). Wider Q = broader, harmonically richer. Changing Q can nudge loop gain, so move it gently and watch the level.
A second EQ notchCarve out a harsh harmonic that builds in S3 — tames the scream into a tone (the tuned-instability idea from page 03).
Stereo width / panPan the return for movement without touching gain. Costs nothing in safety.
Safe-range discipline

Every state above lives below the cap, and the cap lives below true runaway, and the limiter sits below that as a hard backstop. Three layers of margin. The palette is wide because the ceiling is fixed — you can explore freely precisely because you cannot reach the dangerous part.
