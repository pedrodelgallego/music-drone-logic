Feedback recipes with safe ranges

Three loop characters, each a different relationship to unity. The dub wash lives well below it and just thickens; the resonant howl flirts with it; the filtered tail rides toward it and back. Every recipe assumes the master limiter at -3 dBFS and the in-loop filter from Setup are already in place. The ranges are deliberately conservative — start at the low end and creep up.

Read the ranges as ceilings, not targets

Every number below is the point past which the loop gets unpredictable. Start lower. The interesting sound almost always lives at 60–80 % of the listed maximum, with the top of the range reserved for a brief, hands-on gesture.

Recipe 1 — the dub wash

Recipe 1

A thick, evolving smear that never climbs

Character
  • The Basic Channel / Rhythm & Sound space: a chord stab fed into a loop that lives far below unity, so repeats just thicken and blur into a wash, then fade.
  • Safe by design — at these settings you can take your hand off and it always resolves.
Settings & safe ranges
Loop home well below unity
Self-send / feedback~ 40–55 % of unity. Repeats thicken but always decay.
In-loop Utility trim-6 to -4 dB — strong guaranteed decay
Echo time3/16 dotted feel, sync on
In-loop filterHP 200 Hz, LP 3.5–4 kHz
Reverb decay3–4 s, 100 % wet
Saturator drive+2 to +4 dB, Soft Sine — adds age, not climb

Recipe 2 — the resonant howl

Recipe 2

A singing resonant peak just shy of self-oscillation

Character
  • A resonant filter in the loop adds gain at one frequency, so a pitched howl emerges and sustains. The most expressive recipe — and the most dangerous, because resonance is gain.
  • This is the one where the safety fader earns its keep. Live conservatively; the howl wants to run.
Highest-risk recipe

Resonance adds energy at a single frequency, which is exactly the band that runs away first. Keep resonance modest, keep the LP filter active above the howl, and keep your hand on the gain fader the entire time. Never combine high resonance with self-send above ~70 % of unity.

Settings & safe ranges
Resonance and gain held apart
Self-send / feedback~ 50–65 % of unity — lower than the wash, because resonance adds the rest
In-loop filter resonance35–55 %. Above this it self-oscillates; treat 55 % as a hard ceiling.
Filter typeBand-pass or low-pass with resonance; sweep cutoff slowly to "play" the howl's pitch
In-loop Utility trim-6 dB — keep the strongest decay of any recipe
SaturatorOutput ceiling on, drive +3 dB — caps the howl's peak

Recipe 3 — the filtered tail

Recipe 3

Repeats that darken and hollow as they die

Character
  • The classic dub-echo tail: each pass loses high end, so the repeats get progressively darker and more distant before they vanish. This is the recipe that rides closest to unity safely, because the filter guarantees the decay.
  • Automate the in-loop LP cutoff downward as repeats build — the tail "tunnels" away.
Settings & safe ranges
Filter does the limiting, so gain can sit higher
Self-send / feedback~ 70–85 % of unity — the bloom recipe. Reserve the top for the scripted peak gesture.
In-loop LP cutoffStart 4 kHz, automate down to 800 Hz over the bloom — each pass darker
In-loop HP200 Hz fixed — repeats never thicken the low end
In-loop Utility trim-4 dB
Echo time1/8 or 3/16; longer time = more audible per-pass darkening
SaturatorDrive +4 dB, ceiling on — dirt accumulates as the tail decays

Quick comparison

How close each recipe lives to the edge
Dub washFar below unity (40–55 %). Hands-off safe. Thickens, never climbs.
Resonant howlModerate gain (50–65 %) + resonance. Highest risk. Hand on fader always.
Filtered tailClosest to unity (70–85 %), but the filter guarantees decay. The bloom recipe.
Decision check
  • Did you start each recipe at the low end of its range and creep up, or jump to the maximum?
  • For the resonant howl: is resonance below 55 % and your hand on the gain fader?
  • For the filtered tail: is the HP at 200 Hz so the repeats never compound sub?
  • If you removed the in-loop filter right now, which recipe would run away first? (The howl — never run it unfiltered.)
