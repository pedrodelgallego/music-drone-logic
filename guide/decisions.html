02 — Decisions

Key design decisions

Eight choices made up front, before any sound is recorded. Each one shapes everything that follows.

Use very few elements

  • One bass, one drone, one atmosphere
  • One Digitakt kick, one closed hat, one percussion voice
  • Six main elements maximum. Digitakt II gives power, but the course still rewards restraint

Repetition is the engine

  • The main sequence loops for minutes
  • Familiarity is the prerequisite for meaning
  • Once internalised, the smallest change is an event

Modulation creates movement

  • Filter cutoff and resonance changes
  • LFO movement, reverb send, delay feedback
  • Saturation and timbre shifts — not new notes

Atmosphere = rhythm

  • Not background decoration
  • Carries emotional depth, scale, immersion
  • Remove it and the track collapses into a loop

Avoid big drops

  • This is not an EDM track
  • Energy rises and falls gradually
  • The reward is immersion, not surprise

Change one thing at a time

  • One variable per pass while learning
  • You need to know what made the difference
  • Multiple changes destroy diagnostic clarity

Record audio early

  • Commit MicroFreak and Digitakt II passes as audio, not endless live patterns
  • Invites editing, resampling, reversing, slicing, and destructive arrangement choices
  • Each commitment becomes a creative constraint instead of another option to manage

Three tools, three roles

  • Ableton records, arranges, processes, mixes, and exports
  • MicroFreak supplies bass, drone, and unstable synth texture
  • Digitakt II supplies drums, sample manipulation, pattern memory, and performance mutes
Discipline

The discipline is to make small changes feel large.
