Lab Note B8-002
The Release · 05 — Delivery targets

The numbers, the recipe, and the checklist

Three reference tables you will keep open through the build: the delivery targets for each context, a master-chain recipe with starting values, and the release checklist that says when the work is actually done. These are starting points and ceilings, not law — but every number here is a decision you should be able to defend.

Delivery targets by context

Pick the context first, then the target follows. Streaming normalises to roughly −14 LUFS, so mastering hotter only trades dynamics for nothing. Club and long-form can sit quieter and keep their range.

Integrated LUFS & true-peak by destination
Streaming (Spotify, Apple, YouTube)≈ −14 LUFS integrated · true-peak ≤ −1.0 dBTP. Platforms normalise near here; above it they only turn you down.
Apple Music / Apple Digital Masters≈ −16 LUFS integrated · true-peak ≤ −1.0 dBTP. Slightly quieter target; the same dynamics-first logic.
Club / DJ play (long-form, hypnotic)−12 to −9 LUFS is common, but let a slow build keep its range — favour the lower end and protect dynamics. True-peak ≤ −1.0 dBTP still holds.
Loud club / "competitive" single−9 to −8 LUFS at most, and only if the music genuinely wants impact-on-first-listen. Expect to spend dynamic range to get there.
CD / file delivery (16-bit)No platform normalisation — master to what the music wants, true-peak ≤ −1.0 dBTP, dithered to 16-bit. Often the most dynamic master of the set.
The source master (always)24-bit / 48 kHz, un-dithered, Normalize OFF. The source of truth every delivery format is exported from.
Read it as a ceiling, not a goal

The LUFS figure is the loudest you should consider, not a number to hit. If the mix lands at −15 LUFS naturally and sounds finished, do not push it to −14. The quiet you keep is the dynamics you keep.

Master-chain recipe

Starting values for the chain you built on the setup page. Begin here, then move only what the reference A/B and the meter tell you to move.

1 — Corrective EQ Eight
High-pass20–25 Hz, 24 dB/oct — remove sub-rumble that eats limiter headroom and never reproduces
Resonance cutsNarrow (Q ≈ 4–6), only where the mix left a ring. If there are none, do nothing.
MindsetSurgical, inaudible-by-itself. This stage fixes; it does not flavour.
2 — Glue Compressor
Ratio2:1
Attack30 ms — slow enough to let transients through
ReleaseAuto — follows the program
Gain reduction≤ 1–2 dB on the loudest section. Glue, never pump.
3 — Tonal EQ Eight
Low shelf±0.5–1.5 dB to match the reference's low weight
High shelf±0.5–1.5 dB for air — trust the phone and car, not the DT 990's 7–10 kHz lift
CeilingIf any move exceeds ±2 dB, stop and fix the mix instead
4 — Limiter (true-peak)
True-peak limitingON
Ceiling−1.0 dBTP
Input gainRaise only until integrated LUFS reaches target; watch gain reduction stay modest
Lookahead / releaseDefault to start; lengthen release if you hear distortion on sustained low notes
5 — Meter (listens only)
Integrated LUFSYour target ± 0.5 LU after a full-piece pass
True-peakNever exceeds −1.0 dBTP
CorrelationStays positive; brief dips toward 0 are fine, sustained negative is a phase fault

The release checklist

Done means done
  • Every track hits its named integrated-LUFS target within ± 0.5 LU.
  • No track exceeds −1.0 dBTP true-peak anywhere across its full length.
  • Each master translates on monitors/DT 990, phone, car, and in mono.
  • Correlation stays positive through every track; nothing cancels in mono.
  • Tracks sit at a consistent loudness across the EP — no accidental level jumps.
  • Each 16-bit delivery is dithered exactly once; the 24-bit source is un-dithered.
  • The running order plays as one statement, confirmed in a full sitting more than once.
  • The artist statement names the intention and the refusals, not the gear.
  • Positioning names who it is for — and who it is not for.
  • The archive holds masters, sessions, statement, and patches, dated and named.
  • A provenance note records what changed from the spec and why.
  • I can explain why every master move was made.
  • The checklist is the contract. If any line is unchecked, the release is not finished — it is in progress. Print it, work down it, and only ship when every line is true.
