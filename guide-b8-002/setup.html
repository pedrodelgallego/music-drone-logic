The Release · 04 — Setup & rig

The mastering rig and its signal path

Mastering is mostly a listening problem, not a plugin problem. The rig is small: a clean master-bus chain, one trustworthy LUFS meter, a reference track sitting alongside your piece at matched level, a set of systems to translate on, and an export path that dithers exactly once. This page wires all of it before the build touches a fader.

The mastering signal path

Everything happens on one channel — the master bus of a dedicated mastering set, or a fresh set with your bounced mix on a single track. The order is load-bearing: corrective moves first, the loudness stage near the end, the meter listening after everything, and dither only at export.

Bounced mix (24-bit) → Corrective EQ → Glue compression → Tonal EQ → Limiter (true-peak) → LUFS / dBTP / correlation meter → export → Dither (16-bit only)
The meter listens last

Put the metering device after the limiter, so it reads what actually leaves. The limiter's own true-peak ceiling is the safeguard; the meter is the verification. Never trust a number from before the last processing stage.

Build the master-bus chain in Live

A dedicated mastering set

  • New Live set, one audio track, your bounced 24-bit / 48 kHz mix imported
  • Warp off — never time-stretch a finished mix
  • All processing on the master chain, not the track, so the meter reads the true output
  • Buffer at 512+; latency is irrelevant, CPU headroom is not

One LUFS meter, ready first

  • Live 12 Suite ships a metering display with LUFS, or install Youlean Loudness Meter 2 (free)
  • Either reads momentary, short-term, and integrated LUFS plus true-peak
  • Place it last on the master chain, after the limiter
  • Have it loaded before step 1 of the build — you cannot target a number you cannot see
Master-bus devices, in order
1 — EQ Eight (corrective)Surgical only: a high-pass below the lowest useful frequency (≈ 20–25 Hz), and narrow cuts of any resonance the mix left behind. Nothing musical yet.
2 — Glue CompressorGentle bus glue: ratio 2:1, slow attack 30 ms, auto release, threshold for ≤ 1–2 dB of reduction on the loudest section. Glue, not pumping.
3 — EQ Eight (tonal)Broad, gentle shelves to match the reference balance — a fraction of a dB at a time. If you need more than ±2 dB anywhere, fix the mix instead.
4 — LimiterLive's Limiter with true-peak limiting enabled. Ceiling −1.0 dBTP. Push input gain only until the integrated LUFS reaches target; watch gain-reduction stay modest.
5 — LUFS / dBTP / correlation meterReads the final output. No processing here — it only listens.
  • The chain is short on purpose. A finished mix that needs heavy mastering is a mix that is not finished. Most of the work here is verification, not correction.
  • Dither is not on this chain. It lives inside the 16-bit export only — see the export section below. The 24-bit master never gets dithered.

The reference-track method

Set up an honest A/B
Import the referenceDrop your chosen lossless reference onto a second track in the mastering set, routed past the master chain (or on a separate set you switch to) so it is heard raw, not through your processing.
Level-match itPull the reference's track gain down until its loudness matches your master by ear and on the LUFS meter. The louder track always wins the first impression — remove that bias before judging.
A/B in short burstsSwitch between reference and master every few seconds, on the same section type. Listen to broad balance — sub, low-mid, mid, top — not detail.
Match balance, not materialMove your tonal EQ toward the reference's balance in fractions of a dB. Never chase its arrangement, its sounds, or its exact loudness.

The translation rig

A master is verified on systems, not opinions. Audition every master on at least four, in this order — the harshest honest test is the cheap mid-heavy speaker.

SystemWhat it revealsHow to use it
Monitors or DT 990 ProThe detailed, full-range picture you mixed on — and the DT 990's 7–10 kHz lift, which will tempt you to over-cut highsMake decisions here, then verify everywhere else. Trust the others when they disagree about the extremes.
Phone speakerMid-range honesty: anything that lives only in sub or only in air vanishes. The harshest real-world test.If the groove and the hook survive a phone speaker, the balance is robust.
Car or earbudsHow most listening actually happens — and a different bass coupling than any roomCheck the low end is present but not boomy, and the vocal/lead is intelligible.
Mono fold-downPhase and width problems: a wide drone or stereo-widened low end can cancel and disappearSum to mono (Utility Width 0 % on the master, briefly) and confirm nothing essential drops out. Watch the correlation meter stay positive.
The translation rule

If the piece only works at one volume on one system, it is not mastered yet. It must hold its shape quiet and loud, on a phone and in a car, in stereo and in mono. Note one thing the reference keeps everywhere — your master must clear the same bar.

The export & dither path

Two exports from one master
1 — The source masterExport the master chain to WAV · 48 kHz · 24-bit · Normalize OFF · Dither OFF. This un-dithered file is the source of truth; everything else derives from it.
2 — The 16-bit deliveryExport again to WAV · 44.1 or 48 kHz · 16-bit · Dither ON. Live applies dither only because the bit depth drops here — this is the single, last dither stage.
Lossy formatsGenerate MP3/AAC from the 24-bit source or the 16-bit file with the platform's encoder, not by re-dithering. Never dither twice.
FilenamesVersion- and date-tag everything: ep_track1_master_24b_v1.wav, ep_track1_16b_v1.wav. The archive depends on names you can trust later.
Rig ready check
  • Is the LUFS / dBTP / correlation meter loaded last on the master chain, reading the true output?
  • Is the limiter's true-peak ceiling set to −1.0 dBTP before I push a single dB of gain?
  • Is the reference imported and level-matched down to my master?
  • Do I have four systems to translate on, including a phone speaker and a mono fold-down?
  • Is my export plan one 24-bit un-dithered source and one 16-bit dithered delivery — dither exactly once?
