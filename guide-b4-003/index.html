Lab Note B4-003
Granular · 01 — Overview

Granular synthesis & slicing as the engine

Take one short recording, chop it into windows of a few dozen milliseconds, and reassemble those windows into clouds the source never played. Freeze a single instant into a held chord. Slice a phrase at its transients and re-sequence it into a new rhythm. Resample the result and start again. This Lab Note treats granular processing as the whole instrument — a textural piece grown from one source cloud, not a granular preset dropped onto a finished track.

The goal

A textural original from one cloud

  • A 6–8 minute piece grown from a single recorded source — one drone note, one struck string, one vowel
  • Every layer is a generation of that source: granulated, frozen, sliced, resampled
  • Fully original — the source is yours and the grammar is yours; no reference track is copied

Fundamentals you'll practice

  • The four grain controls: size, position, density, spray — and freezing
  • Slicing by transient and reassembling slices into a pattern the source never played
  • Resampling a granular result back as new material — generations stacked on tape logic
Core objective

Understand how which slice of time enters the grain engine — not how loud the source is — becomes the load-bearing sound-design decision. Position is the new gain.

Why this exercise matters

The beginner instinct

  • Drop a granular preset on a finished sound and turn the wet knob up
  • Load a longer, busier sample so there is "more to granulate"
  • Treat the stutter and the click as glitches to fix rather than material to compose with

The opposite discipline

  • Start from one short, well-chosen source and earn every layer from it
  • Move position and size slowly; let the cloud evolve over minutes, not bars
  • Commit each transformation by resampling — and write down what entered each generation

Lab Note B4-003 sits behind Block 4 — transformation systems. It narrows that block to one engine: the grain. The skill transfers anywhere a sound needs to be decoupled from its own timeline — ambient, musique concrète, sound design, generative patches, film scoring, live texture performance.

Principle

Granular decouples time from pitch. Once a sound no longer has to play forward at its own speed, a half-second of audio becomes a whole piece.
