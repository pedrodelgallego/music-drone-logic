Lab Note B6-002
Self-playing Patches · 01 — Overview

Build a patch that plays itself while you leave the room

A deep-reference Lab Note on Eno-style systems that run without you — several slow modulators cross-modulating at rates that never line up, so the combined motion never repeats inside the listening window. You design the habitat; the system plays the music. The deliverable is a ten-minute unattended recording you had no hand in performing. Fully original; no melodies or arrangements borrowed.

The goal

A ten-minute self-player

  • A patch built in VCV Rack, the MicroFreak matrix, or Live + Max for Live
  • Two or more slow modulators at unrelated, unsynced rates
  • Captured as a single hands-off take — your hands never touch it once it starts

Skills you'll practice

  • Cross-modulation: one LFO shaping another LFO's rate or depth
  • Choosing prime-ish, unrelated periods so cycles never realign
  • Deciding what to fix and what to let move; the ten-minute unattended test
Core objective

Understand how a handful of slow modulators, set once and left alone, can sustain ten minutes where no thirty-second window sounds quite like another — yet the whole thing clearly comes from one habitat.

Why self-playing patches matter

The performer's instinct

  • Ride the knobs constantly to "keep it interesting"
  • Treat every dull moment as a cue to intervene
  • Confuse your own boredom with the system's failure

The designer's discipline

  • Set the rates, depths, and range once, then walk away
  • Fix the boundaries; let the system decide the moments
  • If you have to babysit it, it isn't self-playing yet — redesign

This skill transfers far beyond ambient. The same instinct — design a process, then let it run — drives generative installation work, long-form film scoring, modular performance, and any music meant to evolve without a hand on it. You are not learning a genre; you are learning to compose the conditions and let the music live in them.

Principle

Two slow modulators at unrelated rates produce a third motion neither of them contains. That emergent third thing is the music. Your job is to design it, then leave.

How to read this Lab Note

  • 03 Decisions — the theory: phasing vs. cross-modulation, why rates must be unsynced, and what to fix versus what to let move.
  • 04 Setup — three habitats and their signal paths: VCV Rack, the MicroFreak matrix (plus optional Strega), and Live + Max for Live.
  • 05 Palette — modulation-habitat recipes with exact rates and depths you can dial in and trust.
  • 06 The build — the deep page: an exhaustive, step-by-step construction of a self-playing patch and the ten-minute unattended capture.
  • 07 Reflection — fault-finding, what to carry forward, and the single principle that survives the session.
