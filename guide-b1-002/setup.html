Unity · 04 — Setup

The rig, wired for clean gain

A clean gain structure starts with clean wiring. This page sets up the MOTU M4 and a Live template where every stage begins at unity, so the only level you set is the one you mean to. Get this right and the build page becomes a matter of turning one knob and reading one meter.

Ableton Live 12 Suite

  • The whole digital side of the path lives here
  • Meters in dBFS, clip gain, Utility, faders, master
  • Every downstream stage starts at unity in the template

MOTU M4

  • The boundary between analog and digital — and the one trim that matters
  • MicroFreak mono into input 1; Digitakt stereo into 3/4
  • Per-input clip LEDs warn before the converter overs

Arturia MicroFreak

  • The mono source we route from zero to master
  • Its own master volume is the first stage in the path
  • One cable, one trim, one meter — the simplest case to learn on

Elektron Digitakt II

  • The stereo source — already a mix, so a pair of inputs
  • Its Main Volume is the one control over both channels
  • Used to practise gain-staging a stereo bus

Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro

  • Open-back, 250 Ω — honest about hiss and harshness
  • Shows the noise floor and clipping laptop speakers hide
  • Fed only from the M4 phones jack, at one marked level

The monitor knob

  • The M4 phones knob — volume to your ears only
  • Marked at one position; never changes the recording
  • Monitoring discipline made physical

Why clean wiring is the whole game

A gain fault and a wiring fault sound the same — both are "it's noisy" or "one side is missing." If the wiring is right and every digital stage starts at unity, then any level problem can only be the one trim you set. Wire it clean, start at unity, and the bench stops lying to you.

  • TS vs TRS. The MicroFreak output is unbalanced, so a plain TS cable is correct. The Digitakt main outs are impedance-balanced — TRS cables there reject hum picked up along the wire, which keeps the noise floor low and your gain decisions honest.
  • Mono for the MicroFreak. One signal, one input. This is the simplest case to learn gain staging on: one trim, one meter, one number to aim.
  • Keep audio and power apart. A faint whine later is almost always an audio cable lying across a power supply or USB hub — a wiring fault dressed up as a gain fault.
  • If your gear differs: keep the roles, not the names. One mono source, one stereo source, one interface, one headphone path, one DAW.

One listening path only

Turn the M4's monitor blend fully to computer playback. If you hear both the direct input and the playback, you get a doubling or flange and you cannot trust what you're hearing. You monitor through Live, through one path, full stop.

  • No limiter on the master yet. A limiter hides clipping by catching it for you, which defeats the lesson. You want to see the level and leave the headroom yourself. The limiter is a Block 5 conversation.
  • Put a meter where you'll look. A metering plug-in on the master, set to show peaks, is the dashboard for the whole build. You will glance at it after every stage.
