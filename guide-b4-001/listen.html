Lab Note B4-001
Resonance · 02 — Listen

Hear the filter sing first

Before you touch a knob, train your ear on records where the resonant filter is the lead. These are the canonical replication studies for the acid lineage. Work through the linked rep lessons in the workshop, but listen with one question above all: when does the filter stop being a color and become a melody?

The three core studies

Each links to a full replication lesson in block-4. Listen once for pleasure, once with the question in the right-hand column, once while watching a spectrum analyser so you can see the resonant peak move.

Study 01

Hardfloor — “Acperience 1”

Why it is the reference
  • The 303 filter ride as a long-form arc: a single resonant line opens over many minutes, and the arrangement is the cutoff envelope.
  • Two intertwined acid lines prove the filter can carry both melody and counterpoint — listen to how rarely a non-filter event happens.
  • The resonance never tips fully into screech; it rides the 70–95% rung where the peak dominates but stays musical.
Listen for
  • The moment the accent + slide pattern makes the cutoff sound played rather than swept.
  • Full lesson: rep-hardfloor-acperience-1.html
Study 02

Josh Wink — “Higher State of Consciousness”

Why it is the reference
  • The most famous filter ride in dance music: the hook is literally a resonance sweep, not a note sequence.
  • The peak is pushed so hot it distorts the next stage — resonance and saturation become one gesture (carry that forward to the build).
  • Tension is built almost entirely by widening the resonance and dragging the cutoff up, then releasing both.
Listen for
  • How the resonant peak's loudness is itself an arrangement tool — it gets louder relative to everything as the energy rises.
  • Full lesson: rep-josh-wink-higher-state-of-consciousness.html
Study 03

Emmanuel Top — “Acid Phase”

Why it is the reference
  • Patience as a technique: the resonant line evolves over an extraordinarily long span, one tiny cutoff change at a time.
  • It teaches restraint — the filter is high-Q throughout, yet it never tires the ear because the cutoff moves so slowly.
  • The clearest lesson in "the ride is the song": remove the filter automation and there is almost nothing left.
Listen for
  • The slowest cutoff ramps in the whole study — time them. They are far longer than feels natural.
  • Full lesson: rep-emmanuel-top-acid-phase.html

Wider listening — the lineage and its edges

Other rep lessons in block-4 that bear on resonance-as-voice
Charanjit Singh — Raga BhairavThe 303 before acid existed: a resonant filter playing scales. Proof the technique is melodic, not just textural.
Tin Man — NonneoSoft, sustained acid. The resonance is dialed back so the line sings rather than bites — the gentle end of the same ladder.
Luke Vibert — I Love AcidPlayful, accent-driven filter writing — the slide and accent are the phrasing of the filter as an instrument.
Plastikman — PlasticityResonance used minimally and hypnotically — how little movement it takes to carry a long track.
Listening discipline

Solo the filter line in your head. Mute everything else mentally and ask: could you hum it? In every record above the answer is yes — the resonant peak crosses from color into pitch. That crossing is the whole subject of this Lab Note, and the thing you must hear before you can build it.

  • Originality constraint: study these to learn the mechanism, never to copy the line. Your filter ride must be your own — a different scale, a different cutoff shape, a different source feeding the filter. Replicate the technique on the workshop pages; invent the music here.
