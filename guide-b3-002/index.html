Probability · 01 — Overview

Probability as the engine of a pattern that never quite repeats

A deep-reference Lab Note on conditional events — Digitakt II trig conditions (%, A:B, PRE/NEI, FILL) used as logic rather than as a randomize button. The goal: a loop that stays coherent bar after bar yet never falls into exact repetition. Fully original; no melodies or arrangements borrowed.

The goal

A loop that breathes

  • A fixed skeleton that always plays — kick, the spine of the groove
  • One or two uncertain hits, each with a chosen probability and a known place
  • Surprise that lands against a stable, recognisable bar

Conditions you'll master

  • Percentage (%) for genuine surprise; A:B for periodic phrasing
  • PRE and NEI for chains — events that cause other events
  • FILL as a performable second state of the same pattern
Core objective

Understand how a small set of conditional rules can keep a single 16-step pattern alive for eight minutes without ever copying a second pattern, and without dissolving into noise.

Why probability matters here

Randomness as noise

  • "Make everything 50 % random" applied across the kit
  • The pattern dissolves into a haze with no anchor
  • Surprise with no structure to surprise against

Controlled randomness

  • Most of the bar is certain; a tiny budget is uncertain
  • Each uncertainty has a place, a probability, and a reason
  • The listener can't predict the next bar, yet it's the same piece

This skill transfers far beyond techno. The same instinct — a stable body plus a measured dose of uncertainty — drives generative music, footwork, dub, and any long-form piece that has to hold attention without constant new material. You are not learning a feature menu; you are learning to compose with conditions.

Principle

A loop repeats. A conditional loop never quite repeats, but always could. That gap is where life lives.

How to read this Lab Note

  • 02 Listen — three reference recordings that demonstrate probability and conditional logic as a compositional engine.
  • 03 Decisions — the theory: independent vs. periodic events, bounded vs. unbounded randomness, where the budget for uncertainty should go.
  • 04 Setup — the rig and signal path; every trig condition on the Digitakt II, plus the Live MIDI and Max for Live tools that mirror them.
  • 05 Palette — one worked conditional pattern, built condition by condition, so you can hear what each rule adds.
  • 06 The build — the deep page: an exhaustive, step-by-step probabilistic track from empty project to render.
  • 07 Reflection — fault-finding, what to carry forward, and the single principle that survives the session.
