Lab Note B5-002
Reverb · 01 — Overview

Reverb as the space the track lives in — and the form it moves through

A deep-reference Lab Note on reverb treated as place and as blur, not as a "wet" knob bolted onto a finished mix. The goal: a 6–8 minute original where the reverb is the composition — pre-delay, decay, and size define the room, and the swelling and stripping of that room defines the arrangement. Fully original; no melodies, samples, or arrangements borrowed.

The goal

A piece built from space

  • A small set of sources — kick, bass, drone, one texture — placed at different depths in one room
  • The room itself grows and shrinks; that motion is the structure
  • A frozen reverb tail captured as material, not left as a live effect

What you'll master

  • Pre-delay, decay (RT60), and size as three independent dials, not one "amount"
  • The place-vs-blur decision, and the send-vs-insert decision that follows it
  • Freezing tails and printing them; high-passing and ducking the room so it adds depth without weight
Core objective

Understand how the dimensions of a single reverb — when its tail starts, how long it rings, how large it reads — can place a handful of sounds at different distances and then carry an eight-minute arc on their own.

Why reverb matters here

Reverb as garnish

  • A reverb dropped on at the end to "glue" a mix that was never spaced
  • One amount knob raised until things sound distant, then left there
  • A washed-out, muddy result no EQ can rescue

Reverb as space

  • The room is decided first, then sources are placed in it deliberately
  • Each sound has a distance — some near and dry, some far and blurred
  • The space moves, and the listener notices depth rather than effects

This skill transfers far beyond techno. Spatial thinking — near vs. far, place vs. blur, a single coherent room — drives ambient, film scoring, dub, drone, and any music where depth carries as much meaning as melody. You are not learning a plug-in; you are learning to compose with distance.

Principle

Decide whether you are placing a sound or blurring it before you touch a knob. Placement and blur pull every parameter in opposite directions.

How to read this Lab Note

  • 02 Listen — three reference recordings where reverb defines both the space and the form of the music.
  • 03 Decisions — the theory: pre-delay, decay, and size; the place-vs-blur intent; the wet/dry ambiguity; send vs. insert.
  • 04 Setup — the rig and signal path; Ableton's Hybrid Reverb on sends, the freeze-and-resample workflow, and how the room is wired.
  • 05 Palette — four reverb recipes (room, plate, hall, freeze) with exact pre-delay and decay values to dial and audition.
  • 06 The build — the deep page: an exhaustive, step-by-step reverb-defined track from empty project to render.
  • 07 Reflection — fault-finding, what to carry forward, and the single principle that survives the session.
