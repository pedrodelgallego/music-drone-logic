Subtractive · 04 — Setup

The MicroFreak from init, into a clean signal

One voice work asks almost nothing of the rig — one synth, one cable, honest monitoring. But it asks two things absolutely: that you start from a true init patch so every change is yours, and that the signal is clean and well-gained so you are judging the sound, not a converter or a clip. This page wires exactly that, and nothing more.

Arturia MicroFreak

  • The one voice — one engine, one analog filter, one amp envelope
  • Started from a true init patch, then built one control at a time
  • Mod matrix holds the single modulator that makes it evolve

MOTU M4

  • The single audio device — MicroFreak mono into input 1
  • Gain set by the Lab Note B1-002 / B1-003 method, never by eye
  • Extra headroom budgeted because a held tone is hot at the same peak

Ableton Live 12 Suite

  • Records the voice; arranges by subtraction and by level
  • Holds Analog / Operator as a software fallback for the same patch
  • No effects on the voice — the tone must stand dry

Monitoring

  • Your calibrated headphones at your calibrated level
  • One monitoring path declared in one line — direct or software
  • Ear breaks scheduled — sustained tones tire you fast

Software fallback

  • Live's Analog — two oscillators, classic LP/HP filter, ADSR; use one osc
  • Live's Operator in subtractive mode — one carrier, the filter, an LFO
  • Same blocks, same drill — init the device and build from one oscillator

No clock needed

  • No MIDI clock, no sequencer for the drone half — the transport stays stopped
  • The LFO runs free, unsynced, at its own slow rate
  • Notes are held by hand or by the MicroFreak's Hold button

Reach a true init patch first

Before any building, get the synth to a state where you hear the raw oscillator and nothing else. Everything you do afterward is then a change you made and can name. Do not skip this — building on top of a preset's hidden decisions is the single most common reason a beginner cannot say what their own patch is doing.

Get to init on the MicroFreak — confirm each line by ear
  • Pick one engine and leave it. Set Wave and Timbre to a plain mid position — a clean, bright raw tone.
  • Filter wide open: Cutoff fully up, Resonance at zero, type LP. The filter must be removing nothing.
  • Amp envelope neutral: Attack 0, Decay short, Sustain full, Release short. A held note simply turns on and stays on.
  • No modulation: LFO amount zero, the Cycling Envelope off, the Matrix cleared of routings.
  • Glide 0, Paraphonic OFF — one voice only.
  • Hold a note. You should hear a steady, unshaped raw tone. If you hear movement, brightness sweeps, or shaping, something is still routed — clear it before you go on.

  • Why mono. One voice is one mono signal. Recording it mono keeps the decision honest — width, if you want it later, is something you add deliberately, not something that hides a thin tone.
  • Nothing else on the bench. The constraint starts at the wall. For this note there is no second synth and no drum machine — physically set them aside so you are not tempted to solve a dull voice by adding a part.

Gain and headroom — the one measurement to respect

Set the gain on the body of the tone, not its first moment
  • Set the M4 preamp using the method from Lab Note B1-002 and Lab Note B1-003 — by the meter, against a known target, never by ear alone.
  • For a held tone, set the gain on the settled sustain level, not on the opening swell. A drone that swells in will overshoot if you gain it on its first loud moment.
  • Budget extra headroom. A sustained tone delivers far more energy than a spiky source at the same peak reading — most people land at a peak target of −15 dBFS or lower for drones, against the higher target a kick allows.
  • Confirm a held two-minute note never creeps into clipping as the LFO pushes the level up and down. If it does, lower the preamp and re-check on the body of the tone.
Peak targets — spiky vs sustained material
Drums / plucks (spiky)Tall peaks, near-silence between — the higher peak target you calibrated for transient sources holds.
Held tone / drone (sustained)Body sits just under the peak the whole time — far more energy at the same reading. Aim −15 dBFS or lower.
Where to read itOn the settled sustain, after any swell-in and after the LFO has cycled at least once.

The software fallback, set the same way

  • Live's Analog. Drop it on a MIDI track, then init it: use one oscillator (turn OSC 2 off), filter wide open, ADSR neutral, LFO amount zero. You now have the same init patch in software, with the same three blocks.
  • Live's Operator (subtractive mode). Use a single carrier with a saw or square wave, engage the filter section, route the filter envelope and one LFO. The routing names differ from the MicroFreak's matrix, but the blocks are identical.
  • Same drill either way. Whether hardware or software, you reach init, then move one control at a time. The Lab Note's pedagogy does not depend on the MicroFreak — it depends on starting from nothing and naming every change.
