Lab Note B8-001
Spec & Voice · 07 — Reflection

The fault, the carry-forward, the principle

A spec is only as good as the honesty of the review that follows it. Read your finished piece against the document you wrote, find where the two disagree, and decide whether the fault was in the piece or in the spec. Answer slowly. Write the answers down — the spec is a living file, and this is the edit pass.

How you know it worked

Success signals
  • Every line of the spec sorts cleanly into System or Engineering.
  • Each constraint is a number or a refusal that could be caught being broken.
  • The refusal list costs you a tool others rely on.
  • The thesis sentence is falsifiable — you can describe the piece that would betray it.
  • The piece kept the constraints, or showed you which were written too loosely.
  • The energy arrived by opening, not by adding.
  • A stranger holding only the thesis could tell the piece obeys it.
  • The System Spec would still stand on a borrowed rig.
  • You can name one thing the spec forced you to do that improved the work.
Failure signals
  • Lines straddle System and Engineering, so a gear change would rewrite your intentions.
  • A constraint is a mood — "make it hypnotic" — that cannot be broken.
  • The refusal list costs you nothing you would actually reach for.
  • The thesis is a slogan no piece could fail.
  • The voice depends on one synth patch, so it is a sound and not a voice.
  • The piece needed a late arrival to stay alive — movement was not in the system.
  • You broke a constraint at 2 a.m. and called it instinct rather than a decision.
  • The crutch from the audit reappeared, unexamined, in the new piece.

Eleven reflection questions

  1. Which constraint did the most work — forced the most decisions?
  2. Which refusal cost you the most, and was it worth it?
  3. Which line straddled System and Engineering until you forced it into one file?
  4. Did the thesis sentence survive the falsifiability test, or was it a slogan?
  5. Which recurring move from the archive made it into the spec, and which did you leave behind?
  6. Where did the piece and the spec disagree — and was the fault in the piece or the document?
  7. Did a stranger holding the thesis recognise the work as obeying it?
  8. Was your crutch named and dealt with, or did it slip back in?
  9. Could you hand the spec to someone else and get back something recognisably yours?
  10. Would the System Spec stand on a rig you do not own?
  11. What one line will you change in the spec before the next piece?

Carry it forward

  • The fault is usually in the document. When the piece felt wrong, the cause was almost always a constraint written loosely — not a knob set wrong. Tighten the spec, not just the mix.
  • The spec is the deliverable. The piece is one output; the spec is the generator behind every future output. Keep it next to the audio and treat it as the more valuable file.
  • A voice is a constraint you stopped resenting. The refusals that felt like limits this week become, across a year of pieces, the only way you would want to work. The three-year arc of your theses is the real portrait.
Final principle

A spec encodes a voice: what you keep reappearing to, what you refuse, and the one sentence that makes it falsifiable. Author the document, and the work becomes recognisably yours before a single note is played.
