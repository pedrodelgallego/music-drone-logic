Spec & Voice · 05 — Palette

The spec template and the voice-audit worksheet

Two reusable instruments. The spec template is the blank form you fill before any piece. The voice-audit worksheet is the lens you turn on your finished archive to find the move you cannot stop making. Both are shown blank and then filled with a worked example, so you can see the difference between a slogan and a rule with teeth.

Instrument one — the spec template

Copy this into a plain text file. The two halves stay separate on purpose — System above, Engineering below. If a line will not sit cleanly in one half, it is written at the wrong altitude.

Template

The blank form

System Spec — what must be true of the result
Fill before touching the rig
Thesis sentence"I make ___ in which ___ does the work, while refusing ___."
Felt resultWhat should happen in the listener's body?
Load-bearing variableMaterial / Signal / Control / Time / Decision / Human / Archive — pick one
Constraints (3–6)Each a number or a refusal you could be caught breaking
Refusal listWhat is off-limits, stated as plainly as what is allowed
Success signalHow a stranger could tell it worked
Engineering Spec — how the rig produces it
Fill while building, revise when gear changes
RigThe boxes, the interface, the controller
Clock masterExactly one. Named here.
Track listOne per budgeted element, named by role
Parameter rangesThe load-bearing controls and their Min/Max
Movement mapWhat is printed (recorded) vs drawn (automation)
Device listEvery effect, so "stock only" is auditable
Worked example

The same form, filled

System Spec — worked
A long-form drift system
Thesis sentence"I make long-form electronic music where time feels suspended because a few elements drift, refusing every dramatic event."
Felt resultThe listener stops tracking the clock; the room feels larger than the speakers.
Load-bearing variableTime — slow evolution carries the work.
ConstraintsMax five elements; all change is modulation or automation; no element after 3:00; no transition under eight bars; stock effects only.
Refusal listNo drop, no riser, no white-noise sweep, no key change, no effect I cannot justify.
Success signalA stranger cannot point to the moment the energy peaked — it arrived by opening, not by adding.
Engineering Spec — worked
The same system on the workshop rig
RigAbleton, MicroFreak, Digitakt II, MOTU M4, Launchpad Pro MK3
Clock masterAbleton sends sync; both boxes receive only
Track listFOUNDATION, VOICE, SPACE, TEXTURE, ACCENT
Parameter rangesVOICE filter 600 Hz–3.5 kHz; reverb send −10 to −3 dB
Movement mapPrinted: MicroFreak LFOs, Digitakt p-locks. Drawn: Auto Filter, sends, Saturator.
Device listHybrid Reverb, Echo, EQ Eight, Auto Filter, Saturator, Utility — all stock
  • Notice how literally each System line maps down. "Max five elements" became a five-track list; "all change is modulation" became the movement map; "stock only" became a device list you can audit.

Instrument two — the voice-audit worksheet

You cannot write a true thesis from imagination — only from the archive. This worksheet turns finished work into evidence. Run it over every piece you have made in the workshop, then read down the columns for what recurs.

Worksheet

The blank columns

One row per finished piece
Fill honestly — what you did, not what you meant
Defining moveThe single gesture that makes the piece what it is
What carried itWhich layer did the work — Material, Time, Control…
What I refusedThe obvious move I declined, even if unconsciously
What I reached for too easilyThe crutch — the thing I add when unsure
Would a stranger know it was mine?Yes / no / only with the others
Worked example

Three pieces audited

Read down the "defining move" column for the habit
Piece → defining move → what carried it
Block 3 — rhythm studyOne filter opening the whole arrangement; Control
Block 5 — texture pieceA slow swell that never resolves; Time
Block 7 — live takeAn intro that refuses to start for ninety seconds; Time
Piece → what I refused → my easy crutch
Block 3Refused a drop; reached for reverb when unsure
Block 5Refused resolution; reached for reverb when unsure
Block 7Refused a dramatic arrival; reached for reverb when unsure
  • The recurrence: patience and a withheld arrival appear in every row. That is the habit — the raw material of the voice.
  • The crutch: reverb-when-unsure also appears in every row. That is not a signature; it is a tell. Either justify it in the spec or add it to the refusal list.
  • The conversion: name the recurring move ("the withheld arrival"), refuse its opposite ("no dramatic event"), and you have the spine of the thesis from page 03.
  • Habit reads down the columns; signature reads across the spec. The worksheet finds what recurs; the template is where you decide to mean it.
  • The crutch is as informative as the signature. What you reach for when unsure is a refusal waiting to be written. The audit surfaces both.
  • "Only with the others" is the honest answer for a young voice. A single piece rarely declares the voice; the pattern across the archive does. That is why you audit the set, not the favourite.
Decision check
  • Did I fill the worksheet from what I actually did, not what I intended?
  • Does one move recur across most rows — and can I name it in three words?
  • Did a crutch recur too, and have I decided to justify or refuse it?
  • Could I now fill the thesis line of the template from this audit alone?
