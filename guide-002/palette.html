03 — Sound palette

Sound palette

Four layers and a texture bed — all grown from one MicroFreak patch. Each layer is named for its job, because with no melody the job is all a layer has.

Foundation

  • The root pitch, low register — the floor the piece stands on
  • Long attack, full sustain; two slow unsynced modulators inside
  • Present from the first second to the last

Interval layer

  • The same patch at the chosen interval, slightly detuned
  • Its beating against the foundation is the piece's pulse
  • Panned away from the foundation; must survive a mono check

Air layer

  • The same patch two octaves up, filtered to a whisper
  • Heard as light on the ceiling, not as a note
  • Felt more than heard — the Lab Note B1-001 atmosphere rule applies

Texture bed — Digitakt II

  • A sampled fragment of the foundation, played back long and low
  • Track LFO opens its filter over minutes; resample a generation deeper for grain
  • The erosion layer — it supplies dirt, never rhythm

Effects — Ableton stock only

Stock effects are sufficient and they force focus. Same rule as Lab Note B1-001 — only the jobs change.

EffectUsed for
EQ EightHigh-passing each layer into its register; carving so the layers interlock
Hybrid ReverbThe room the piece lives in — decay long, blooming mid-piece
EchoUnsynced time, low feedback — smear, never rhythm
UtilityLevel rides, width, and the mono check that the beating must survive
SaturatorMass and erosion in the final third — density without new layers
SpectrumEyes on the partials: watch what the slow modulators actually move
Chorus-EnsembleOptional, on the air layer only — never on the foundation

Digitakt II — texture engine, not drum machine

Every sequencing feature the machine is famous for stays switched off. What remains is a deep stereo sampler with a slow LFO — exactly what a drone needs.

Digitakt II featureCourse use
Stereo samplingCapture 30–60 s of the MicroFreak foundation take via the external inputs
Sample playbackOne trig, length maxed, loop on — the sample becomes a held tone again
Track LFOSlowest speed, small depth, on filter cutoff — minutes-long movement inside the bed
ResamplingRe-record the bed through its own filter and reverb for a darker generation; each generation adds grain
Pitch / sample startDrop the fragment an octave; nudge start for a softer entry
Trig conditions, retrigs, patternsUnused. If the bed acquires a pulse, you have left the brief
One patch, four layers

Foundation, interval, and air are three printed takes of a single saved patch; the texture bed is that same patch sampled and eroded. The piece can never sound incoherent — every layer is the same tone wearing different weight.
