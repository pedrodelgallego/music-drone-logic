Lab Note B2-002
Concrète · 07 — Reflection

Criteria & reflection

Most of the learning happens here, not while cutting. Answer slowly. Write the answers down — the fault you diagnose now is the skill you carry into the next piece.

How you know it worked

Success signals
  • You can describe the piece without naming a single source.
  • The piece travels its one-sentence spine from start to finish.
  • Objects feel placed, not dumped — silence has weight.
  • At least one object recurs as a recognisable motif.
  • Contrasting objects read as contrast; kindred objects blend.
  • Disparate captures sound like they share one room.
  • Gestures have trajectory — they move through the stereo field.
  • The dynamic range is intact; quiet passages are genuinely quiet.
  • You shaped material you recorded, with no borrowed audio.
  • You can explain why each object earns its place.
Failure signals — faults to diagnose
  • It sounds like a sound-effects reel — events with no thread.
  • Every source is instantly nameable when you didn't intend it.
  • The texture bed loops audibly — you can hear the seam.
  • Objects pile up with no silence; nothing has room to be heard.
  • The piece sits in one place instead of travelling the spine.
  • Reverb is doing the composing — wet mush hides weak montage.
  • Clip seams click because edits aren't faded or zero-crossed.
  • It's loud throughout — compression killed the dynamics.
  • You kept an object out of attachment, not because it works.

Diagnose the dominant fault

Most first concrète pieces fail in one of three ways. Find yours — it tells you exactly what to practise next.

FaultWhat it sounds likeThe fix
Causal captureA nameable reel: "door, then footsteps, then a train". The source kept winning.Transform harder (Step 04) and listen reduced before placing. The cut, not the recording, is the composition.
No spineInteresting objects in no order — it could be shuffled and sound the same.Re-state the axis in one sentence; re-sort objects along it; cut anything off-axis.
No airWall-to-wall sound; nothing lands because nothing has silence around it.Delete a third of the gestures. Open gaps. Let single objects breathe.

Reflection questions

  1. Which sound surprised you most when you stopped hearing its source?
  2. Which transformation moved an object furthest from its origin?
  3. Where did you sit on the Schaeffer–Ferrari line, and was it deliberate?
  4. Which object became your motif, and did the recurrence cohere the piece?
  5. Did the texture bed read as a place, or as a loop?
  6. Where did silence do the most work?
  7. Which gesture's drawn trajectory mattered most?
  8. Did contrasting objects actually contrast, or just collide?
  9. Could a listener trace your spine without your help?
  10. What would you capture differently next session?

Carry forward

What transfers to the next Lab Note

Reduced listening is now a tool you own. Every sample you ever load — a kick, a vocal, a synth — can be heard as mass, grain, and motion before it's heard as a "kick". Your palette catalogue is reusable material. And montage with respect for silence is the foundation of every arrangement you will make, in any genre.

Final principle

A sound is not what made it. Once you can hear the object instead of the cause, every sound in the world becomes material — and composition becomes the art of choosing, cutting, and placing.
