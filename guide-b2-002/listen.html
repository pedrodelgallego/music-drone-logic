Lab Note B2-002
Concrète · 02 — Listen

Listen before you record

Five recordings that define the method, in roughly the order it was invented. Listen to each one twice: once trying to name what you hear, once trying not to. The gap between those two listenings is the whole subject of this Lab Note.

The reference set

These are listening models, not templates. You are studying how each composer treats a recorded sound as material — where they keep the source legible, where they bury it, and how they move from object to object. Each links to a full rep lesson in the workshop.

Schaeffer — Étude aux chemins de fer

  • 1948 — the founding study, made of train recordings
  • Hear how locked-groove loops turn an event into rhythm
  • The moment the source still wins, before the method matured

Open rep lesson →

Ferrari — Presque rien No. 1

  • An unedited-sounding dawn on a beach — "almost nothing"
  • The opposite pole: source left fully legible, by design
  • Composition by framing and patient selection, not cutting

Open rep lesson →

Stockhausen — selected work

  • Where concrète meets electronic synthesis and serial control
  • Recorded sound treated with structural rigour, not collage
  • Listen for sound transformed until its origin dissolves

Open rep lesson →

Bayle — selected work

  • The acousmatic tradition: sound projected over many speakers
  • The image-of-sound — gesture and trajectory in space
  • Listen for how an object moves, not just what it is

Open rep lesson →

Parmegiani — De Natura Sonorum

  • The reference masterwork for morphology and pairing
  • Natural and synthetic sounds set against each other
  • The clearest teaching example of object-to-object montage

How to listen to these

PassWhat to doWhat you are training
1 — CausalName the source of every sound. "Train. Wave. Voice. Metal."Noticing how strong the pull toward the source is — and where the composer fights it.
2 — ReducedForbid yourself the source words. Describe only mass, grain, attack, motion, weight.Reduced listening — the core skill of the whole method.
3 — StructuralMark where one object ends and the next begins. Where are the cuts? What rhymes with what?Hearing montage as form, so you can do it on page 06.
Two poles to hold in mind

Chemins de fer and Presque rien mark the extremes: source-erased and source-preserved. Every choice you make on the Decisions page is a position somewhere on the line between them. Pick yours deliberately.

  • Listen on the DT 990 Pro, then on a phone speaker. Concrète lives in detail — grain, room tone, tiny transients — that open headphones reveal and small speakers hide. Knowing what survives a bad speaker tells you which objects carry the piece.
  • Resist the temptation to read about the pieces first. Program notes hand you the source and ruin the first causal-vs-reduced experiment. Listen blind, then read.
