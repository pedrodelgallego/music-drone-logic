Lab Note B2-002
Concrète · 05 — Palette

A catalogue of sound-objects

Your captured material, classified by morphology — not by what made it. This is the model catalogue: build your own version from your own recordings. Every object gets named for its qualities, sorted into gesture or texture, and placed on the organising axis you chose on the Decisions page.

How to catalogue

Open each capture, listen in reduced mode, and fill one row. The rule: describe, never name the source. If you write "door", you have failed the exercise — write what the door sounds like as an object. The label you give it should let you find the right material six steps into a montage without auditioning fifty files.

Gestures — the foreground objects

Impulse & struck objects
OBJ-dull-thudComplex mass · smooth grain · sharp attack, fast decay · fixed motion. A low, woody impact. Source: contact-mic tap on a hollow box. Role: gesture; sits at the noisy end.
OBJ-bright-tickNodal mass · smooth · very sharp impulse · fixed. A high, dry click with no body. Source: fingernail on glass. Role: gesture; punctuation.
OBJ-metal-strikeTonic-complex mass · smooth grain becoming iterative in the tail · struck, long decay · falling motion. A pitched clang that rings and detunes as it fades. Source: struck radiator. Role: gesture; near the pitched end.
OBJ-wood-knockComplex mass · rough grain · struck, medium decay · fixed. Two surfaces meeting once. Role: gesture; mid-axis.
Swept & moving objects
OBJ-scrape-upNodal mass · rough grain · swelling attack · rising motion. A friction sound climbing in brightness. Source: contact mic dragged along a coil. Role: gesture; a transition-maker.
OBJ-zip-fallComplex mass · granular · sharp onset then decay · falling. A descending rattle. Role: gesture; pairs against scrape-up as its mirror.
OBJ-bowed-riseTonic mass · smooth · slow swell · rising then fixed. A pitch bowed into existence. Source: bowed metal edge. Role: gesture bordering on texture — the bridge between the two families.

Textures — the ground objects

Sustained fields
TEX-grey-hissNoisy mass · smooth grain · flat sustained · fixed. A broadband bed with no pitch. Source: distant traffic ambience, high-passed. Role: texture; the noisy anchor of the axis.
TEX-low-rumbleComplex-noisy mass · smooth · flat sustained · slowly undulating. A felt-more-than-heard low field. Source: ventilation drone, contact mic on a pipe. Role: texture; the room's floor.
TEX-water-bedNodal mass · granular · undulating sustained · oscillating. A continuous trickle that never repeats. Source: stream, stereo mics. Role: texture; mid-axis, adds life.
TEX-pure-tonePure-to-tonic mass · perfectly smooth · flat sustained · fixed. A single held pitch with almost no grain. Source: resonating wine glass, sustained. Role: texture; the pitched anchor — the axis's far end.
Read the catalogue as a map, not a list

Notice the spine emerging: TEX-grey-hiss at the noisy end, TEX-pure-tone at the pitched end, and everything else placed between. A piece that travels from one to the other already has its arc. The gestures are the events that mark stations along the way.

Finding contrast and kinship

Once classified, objects suggest their own relationships. Two with shared morphology blend; two that differ on a key axis contrast. Montage is the art of choosing which.

PairingWhat they share / differ onEffect in montage
scrape-up → zip-fallSame grain, opposite motionA complete arc-and-release gesture from two halves
grey-hiss + low-rumbleBoth noisy textures, different bandsBlend into one believable space; layer them as the bed
bright-tick over pure-toneSharp impulse against flat sustainMaximum contrast — the tick reads as an event because the ground is still
metal-strike → bowed-riseBoth pitched, attack vs. swellKinship in mass lets a hard hit dissolve into a held tone
  • Label files now, in the catalogue's language. Rename the WAVs to match the Object IDs. When you are nine cuts deep on the build page, "OBJ-dull-thud" is findable; "ZOOM0042.WAV" is not.
  • Keep a "rejects" folder, don't delete. A sound that's wrong for the spine may be exactly the disruption a later section needs. Concrète material is too expensive to throw away.
  • One sound can become several objects. A 4-second metal strike is a gesture whole, a texture when its tail is looped, and a new gesture when reversed. Catalogue each derived object as its own row.
Before moving to the build
  • Does every object have a morphological name and an axis position? No "door", no "ZOOM0042".
  • Do you have anchors at both ends of your spine, and at least three stations between?
  • Do you have more gestures than textures? Good — gestures are cheap to place, textures are the slow furniture.
  • Can you point to two pairs that contrast and two that blend? If not, your palette is too uniform — capture more.
