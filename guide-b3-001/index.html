Lab Note B3-001
Groove · 01 — Overview

Groove & micro-timing as the engine of a finished piece

A single-topic deep reference. The whole track is carried by feel — by what you decide to do with the distance between where a note lands and where the grid says it should. This Lab Note treats that distance as the one compositional decision everything else hangs on.

The one principle

Principle

The grid is a reference, not a law. Groove is a deviation you can describe — the same offset, every cycle — and if you can't say what it does in one sentence, it is an accident, not a groove.

Everything in this Lab Note is downstream of that sentence. A swing percentage, a tick nudge, an extracted Ableton groove, a hand riding a fader — each is just a way of stating, precisely, how far and in which direction a part leans away from the metronome. State it, repeat it, and the body learns to lean with it.

What you'll build

A groove-driven original

  • One 6–8 minute track whose interest comes from feel, not from new parts
  • Made with Ableton Live 12 Suite, Elektron Digitakt II, and Arturia MicroFreak
  • Fully original — no borrowed patterns; the groove is yours, stated as numbers

The skills you'll practice

  • Swing / shuffle in the 53–58 % pocket; push vs pull
  • Digitakt II per-trig micro-timing nudges in ticks
  • Ableton Groove Pool and Extract Groove — one shared pocket
  • Humanization vs intention — repeatable offset over random jitter

Why feel, not parts

The beginner instinct

  • Quantize everything to the grid, then add parts to fix the stiffness
  • Reach for a "humanize" button and hope randomness reads as feel
  • Treat groove as polish applied at the end, not the engine at the start

The opposite discipline

  • Decide the groove first, as a small set of named offsets
  • Keep the kick as a metronomic reference; let everything else lean against it
  • Make the same loop feel different at 0:30 and 3:00 by feel, not by addition

The skill transfers. The same instinct — name the deviation, repeat it, share it across instruments — carries into house, broken beat, dub, IDM, and any music where the recording must breathe like a body rather than tick like a clock.

How to read this Lab Note

Work the pages in order. Listen trains your ear on four reference grooves. Decisions is the theory. Setup wires the rig for groove work. Palette hands you concrete numbers. The build is the long, exact, step-by-step. Reflection turns the result into a carry-forward rule.
