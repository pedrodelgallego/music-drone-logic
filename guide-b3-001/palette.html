Concrete groove settings

Three ready-to-program groove templates, the exact tick offset for every element, and the swing percentages that put each one in its pocket. Steal a template, then mutate it — but start from real numbers, not from "feel".

The reference numbers

What a tick is worth at 124 BPM
One bar≈ 1935 ms
One 16th step≈ 121 ms
Micro-timing range per trig±23 ticks (≈ ±58 ms, roughly half a step)
One tick≈ 2.5 ms
Felt nudge3–6 ticks (≈ 8–15 ms)
Heard as a flam10+ ticks (≈ 25 ms)
Sign convention used on this page

+ means late (pull, behind the beat). − means early (push, ahead of the beat). The kick is always 0 — it is the reference clock.

Template A — settings per element
Kick (T1)Steps 1, 5, 9, 13 · micro-timing 0 · swing not applied · velocity full
Hat (T2)Offbeat steps 3, 7, 11, 15 · micro-timing +4 · or swing 55 % on this track only · velocity alternating 100 / 80
Perc (T3)Steps 4 and 12 · micro-timing −3 · velocity ~90 · trig condition 50 % on step 12
Swing (pattern)55 % if using global swing instead of per-trig hat nudges — do one or the other, not both
  • One sentence: "Kick on the grid, hats dragged 4 ticks, perc accent pushed 3 ticks early on the bar lead-ins." If you can say that, it's a groove, not an accident.

Template B — Deep drag (heavy, late-night)

Everything except the kick pulled further behind. Reads as weight and exhaustion — the body leaning back. Use sparingly; too much drag and it sounds broken rather than deep.

Template B — settings per element
Kick (T1)Four-on-the-floor · micro-timing 0 · the only thing the listener trusts
Hat (T2)Offbeats · micro-timing +7 · velocity 100 / 70 alternating
Clap / snare (T3)Steps 5 and 13 · micro-timing +5 · the backbeat drags with the hats
Ghost perc (T4)Step 14 only · micro-timing +9 · velocity 55 · trig condition 1:2
Swing (pattern)57 % — the upper edge of the pocket before it reads as shuffle
  • The risk: at +7 to +9 ticks you are near a third of a step late. Audition against the locked kick for two full minutes — if it stops feeling like a lean and starts feeling like a mistake, pull every offset back by 2 ticks.

Template C — Forward push (driving)

Accents land early; the track feels like it is straining forward against the clock. Good for peak sections. The kick still does not move — only the urgency around it.

Template C — settings per element
Kick (T1)Four-on-the-floor · micro-timing 0
Hat (T2)Offbeats · micro-timing −2 · slight forward lean · velocity 100 / 85
Lead perc (T3)Steps 4, 8, 12, 16 · micro-timing −5 · the part that "pulls the track forward"
Off-accent (T4)Step 7 · micro-timing −6 · velocity 95 · trig condition 75 %
Swing (pattern)50–52 % — near-straight; the push, not swing, carries the energy

Swing percentage cheat-sheet

Where each amount sits
50 %Straight. The reference state and Template C's home.
53–54 %The faintest roll. Felt only over many bars.
55 %Template A. The dependable pocket.
57 %Template B. Heavy, on the edge of obvious.
58–62 %House / garage shuffle. Usually too much for hypnotic techno.
66 %Triplet feel. A different genre.

Velocity — the other half of the pocket

  • Couple timing and dynamics. A part that lands late should usually also be a touch quieter. Late + soft reads as a ghost note; late + loud reads as an error.
  • Alternate the offbeat hats. 100 / 80 / 100 / 80 across the four offbeats stops them ticking like a clock. On the Digitakt II, p-lock the AMP volume per trig.
  • Keep the kick constant. The reference clock is constant in both time and level. Velocity variation on the kick undermines the anchor every other part leans on.

Ableton Groove Pool starting values

When applying an extracted groove to the MicroFreak bass
Timing100 % — apply the full extracted offset so the bass shares the exact drum lean
Quantize0 % — do not pre-straighten; you want the recorded feel intact
Velocity0–30 % — let the bass keep its own dynamics unless you want the drum accents imposed
Random0 % on bass; up to 5 % only on a long ghost-hat layer
Base1/16 — the grid the offsets are measured against
The palette rule

Pick one template and commit. Mutating a stated groove (A → B by adding 3 ticks to every part) is composition. Hopping between templates mid-track because none "feels right" is the indecision this Lab Note exists to cure.

Before the build
  • I have chosen one template (A, B, or C) as my starting groove.
  • I have written the tick offset for every element.
  • I know my swing percentage and that I'm using swing or per-trig nudges, not both.
  • Velocity is coupled to timing — late hits are softer.
  • My Groove Pool plan is set: Timing 100 %, Quantize 0 %, Random 0 % on bass.
